# Ember Phase 3-4 Cards

**Character:** Ember  
**Emotion Family:** Fire  
**Arc:** Anger & Redemption  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Date:** March 17, 2026

---

## Overview

Ember's mid-game cards represent the crisis point: the realization that their anger has caused real damage (Phase 3: Scorched Earth), followed by the difficult beginning of wanting to change (Phase 4: Smoldering). These cards introduce defensive and healing mechanics while maintaining Fire's aggressive identity — the challenge is balancing destruction with recovery.

**Design Philosophy:**
- Phase 3 (Scorched Earth) = consequences, defense, aftermath
- Phase 4 (Smoldering) = guilt, vulnerability, tentative change

---

## Phase 3: Scorched Earth

*The fire has burned out. What's left is ash and acknowledgment.*

### Card 9: Ash Fall

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Attack (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 5 |
| **Effect** | Apply 1 "Soot" to enemy (reduces healing received by 3 this combat). |
| **Flavor** | *"The fallout doesn't care who was right."* |

**Design Notes:** Damage with lasting debuff. Soot represents the lasting consequences of anger — wounds that don't heal cleanly. Pairs with Regret (can't heal the damage you've done).

---

### Card 10: Scars

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Defense (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Gain 6 Shield. Gain 1 Heat. |
| **Flavor** | *"The fire taught me what survives."* |

**Design Notes:** Defensive card that maintains Fire identity through heat gain. Represents hardening after trauma — protected but still burning inside. Transition card between aggressive and controlled play.

### Card 11: Regret

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Heal (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 3 |
| **Effect** | Deal 3 damage. If this kills an enemy, heal 5 HP. |
| **Flavor** | *"I can't take back what I did. But I can stop."* |

**Design Notes:** Conditional heal — you must destroy to heal. Represents the cost of recognition: Ember can only begin to heal after acknowledging what their anger destroyed. High risk, high reward.

---

### Card 12: Cooling Embers

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Control (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 1 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Reduce Heat by 2. Gain 4 Shield. |

## Phase 4: Smoldering

*The anger hasn't gone. It's quieter now — and ashamed.*

### Card 13: Trembling Spark

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Primary Emotion (Anger/Shadow) |
| **Energy Cost** | 1 |
| **Damage** | 3 |
| **Effect** | If you have 0-2 Heat, deal +2 damage. |
| **Flavor** | *"My hands shake now. They never used to."* |

**Design Notes:** Rewards low heat — Ember's trying to contain the fire. Represents the frustration of wanting to be angry but knowing the cost. Works best in Smoldering phase, becomes weaker if you return to Blaze.

---

### Card 14: What Have I Done

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Reaction (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 0 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | When you would take damage, reduce it by 3. Then gain 1 Heat and lose 1 HP. |
| **Flavor** | *"I see their face. I see what I became."* |

**Design Notes:** Defensive but costs HP. The guilt manifests physically — protecting yourself hurts because you feel you deserve it. Enables survival while carrying shame.

---

### Card 15: Wanting to Change

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Buff (Fire/Warmth) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Gain "Wanting" — your next Fire card that deals damage also heals your ally for half damage dealt. |
| **Flavor** | *"What if I could be different? What if I didn't have to burn?"* |

**Design Notes:** First true hybrid card (Fire/Warmth blend). Transmutation gateway — Ember's anger is learning to heal instead of destroy. Opens path to Controlled Burn phase.

---

### Card 16: Cautious Flame

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Attack (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 4 |
| **Effect** | If this is your first attack this turn, apply 1 "Vulnerable" (takes +50% damage next hit). |
| **Flavor** | *"A small fire. Careful. Controlled."* |

**Design Notes:** Lower damage than Blaze cards but applies Vulnerable — sets up allies for big hits. Represents Ember learning to work with others instead of alone.

## Emotional Arc Integration

### Phase 3: Scorched Earth

- **Narrative:** Ember sees the wreckage left by their anger. Relationships damaged, trust burned, people hurt.
- **Mechanic:** First defensive cards appear. Heat becomes a liability to manage.
- **Player Feel:** Recovery, consequence, acknowledgment

The Scorched Earth cards teach players:
1. Damage is done — Soot prevents easy healing
2. Defense is necessary — you can't fight your way out of this
3. Cooling Embers is the key — managing heat enables transition to Smoldering
4. Regret forces confrontation — you must face what you destroyed to heal

### Phase 4: Smoldering

- **Narrative:** Ember carries guilt but hasn't let go. They want to change but fear relapse.
- **Mechanic:** Hybrid cards emerge. Low-heat rewards. Ally support becomes possible.
- **Player Feel:** Vulnerable, hopeful, uncertain

The Smoldering cards teach players:
1. Anger can transform — "Wanting to Change" heals instead of harms
2. Low heat is a strength — Trembling Spark rewards containment
3. Guilt costs — "What Have I Done" protects but hurts
4. Vulnerability is power — Cautious Flame sets up team combos

### Transition to Phase 5

Phase 4 should feel like a turning point:
- "Wanting to Change" opens the Warmth pathway
- Trembling Spark becomes stronger as heat decreases
- Cautious Flame enables ally cooperation

Players who master Scorched Earth and Smoldering will have:
- Heat management skills
- Defensive options
- First hybrid cards
- Clear path to Controlled Burn

## Resonance & Synergy

### New Mechanics

**Soot (Debuff):** Reduces healing received by 3 for the rest of combat. Stacks. Represents lasting damage that doesn't heal cleanly.

**Wanting (Buff):** Next Fire attack also heals ally for 50% damage dealt. First step toward Fire/Warmth transmutation.

**Vulnerable (Debuff):** Target takes +50% damage from next attack. Standard vulnerability, enabled by Cautious Flame.

### Heat Curve Adjustment

- **3+ Heat:** +2 damage to all Fire cards (maintained from Phase 1-2)
- **5+ Heat:** No longer triggers "Blaze" penalty in Smoldering phase (Ember has learned control)
- **Cooling Embers:** Reduce Heat by 2 — primary way to manage resonance

### Synergy Cards (Future Phases)

- **Ash Fall + Regret:** Soot ensures Regret's heal condition matters
- **Scars + Cooling Embers:** Stack shields while managing heat
- **Wanting to Change + Bonfire (Phase 5):** Heals allies on big damage
- **Trembling Spark + Controlled Burn (Phase 5):** Low-heat build pays off

## Balancing Notes

- Phase 3 should feel like a necessary recovery — players forced to slow down
- Phase 4 should feel like growth — new options, different playstyle
- Heat management becomes central — Cooling Embers is essential
- "Wanting to Change" opens hybrid decks — gateway to Controlled Burn
