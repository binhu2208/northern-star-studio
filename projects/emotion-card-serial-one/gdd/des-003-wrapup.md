---

## Final 6-Character Roster

The release cast now reads as:

1. **Maya** — reconciliation, perspective, family hurt
2. **Ember** — anger, consequence, responsible power
3. **Wren** — grief, memory, carrying loss
4. **Tide** — trust, vulnerability, connection after betrayal
5. **Frost** — fear, courage, moving despite danger
6. **Bloom** — joy, hope, self-worth, sustainable care

This creates a strong emotional spread across the roster. The cast is no longer weighted only toward surviving pain; it also includes a character centered on renewal, reciprocity, and learning how to stay alive emotionally without performing wellness.

---

## Why Bloom Was Selected Over Hearth

Both concepts are viable and both solve the same top-level need: a **Warmth-primary** sixth character.

Bloom was selected because Bloom gives the release roster the stronger final contrast and the clearer emotional finish:

- **Bloom broadens the emotional palette more distinctly.** Hearth focuses on compassion, care, and boundaries; Bloom adds joy, hope, and self-worth as the missing release-note emotion set.
- **Bloom better counters the roster's existing weight.** Wren, Tide, Frost, Maya, and Ember all begin from pain, fear, betrayal, grief, resentment, or destructive force. Bloom introduces lived joy and sustainable warmth without becoming shallow.
- **Bloom has a sharper release-role identity.** "Performing brightness vs real joy" feels more differentiated from Maya's reconciliation and Tide's vulnerability than Hearth's "care with boundaries," which overlaps more with the broader support/healing space already implied across the cast.
- **Bloom's mechanics read as a cleaner niche.** Glow, Petals, Rooted, and overflow/backlash create a support-combo engine built around pacing and emotional regulation rather than generic protection.
- **Bloom improves roster tone.** As the sixth slot, Bloom helps the full cast say something larger: healing is not only endurance, acceptance, or restraint — it is also rediscovering joy that is honest and sustainable.

In short: **Hearth is solid, but Bloom completes the six-character lineup more memorably.**

## Hearth Status

**Hearth should be retained as an alternate/reference concept.**

Hearth is not discarded. The concept remains useful as:

- backup direction if Bloom needs to be revisited
- future expansion or variant design space
- reference material for Warmth-support/control mechanics
- a thematic cousin to Bloom if the project later wants multiple Warmth-led arcs

---

## Roster Fit Summary

With Bloom added, the six-character cast now covers:

- reconciliation
- anger
- grief
- trust
- fear
- joy / hope / self-worth

That is a much healthier launch spread than stopping at five pain-forward arcs. Bloom gives the cast a true renewal axis and a support-oriented playstyle that still carries internal tension.

Pairwise, Bloom also fits cleanly:

- **with Maya:** forgiveness vs sustainable joy
- **with Ember:** intensity vs regulated warmth
- **with Wren:** grief vs renewal
- **with Tide:** guarded vulnerability vs safe reciprocity
- **with Frost:** fear management vs comfort and reassurance

The result is a roster with distinct emotional identities and better tonal balance.
