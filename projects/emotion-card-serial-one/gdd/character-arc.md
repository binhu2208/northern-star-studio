## 1. Character Backstory

### Who is Maya?

Maya Chen, 34, is a ceramicist who inherited her grandmother's pottery studio three years ago. She hasn't spoken to her younger brother, Leo, in five years — not since their grandmother's funeral, where a bitter argument about the studio's ownership shattered their relationship.

### Why She's Here

Maya arrives at the **Garden of Echoes**, a mysterious place where emotions take physical form. She doesn't know why she's here or how to leave. A gentle guide explains: *"You carry unresolved feelings. Here, you can feel them fully, understand them, and perhaps... release them."*

Maya's goal: Understand what really happened with Leo and find a path forward — whether that's reconciliation, acceptance, or something else entirely.

## 2. Emotional Starting State

### Dominant Emotion: **Resentment** (Shadow Family)

Maya begins with a deck heavily weighted toward Shadow emotions:

**Starting Deck (15 cards):**
- 4x "Unfair Burden" (Resentment — basic Shadow card)
- 3x "What He Said" (Grief — replaying the argument)
- 3x "Should Have Been Different" (Longing — idealized past)
- 2x "I Was Right" (Stubbornness — Fire/Shadow blend)
- 2x "Empty Studio" (Melancholy — loneliness)
- 1x "Grandmother's Hands" (Gratitude — Warmth anchor)

**Emotional Capacity:** 3 plays per turn (limited by inner turmoil)

### Starting Narrative State

Maya believes:
- Leo abandoned the family when things got hard
- She was the only one who cared for Grandmother
- The studio is her burden alone
- Reconciliation would mean admitting she was wrong

## 3. Key Choice Points

### Choice Point 1: The First Memory

**Trigger:** After tutorial encounter, Maya finds a memory orb showing Leo at Grandmother's bedside.

**Available Options (determined by deck):**

| Option | Required Cards | Emotional Cost |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Watch fully** | Any 2 Shadow cards | Gain "Unexpected Detail" (Memory) |
| **Skip it** | 1x "I Was Right" | Gain "Avoidance" (debuff: -1 capacity for 2 turns) |
| **Watch with curiosity** | 1x "Grandmother's Hands" + 1 Shadow | Unlock hidden scene: Leo crying |

**Narrative Impact:**
- Watch fully → See Leo was there more than she remembered
- Skip it → Miss crucial information, strengthen Resentment
- Watch with curiosity → See Leo's grief, first crack in Maya's narrative

**Deck Consequence:**
- Choosing curiosity adds "Conflicting Memory" to deck (enables Complex Emotions later)

---

### Choice Point 2: The Letter

**Trigger:** Maya discovers an unsent letter from Leo, written the week after the funeral.

**Available Options:**

| Option | Required Cards | Outcome |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Read it coldly** | 3x Shadow cards | Gain "Confirmed Anger" (Fire card), lose "Grandmother's Hands" |
| **Read it with pain** | 2x Grief + 1x any | Gain "His Words Hurt" (Shadow/Fire blend), unlock next memory |
| **Can't read it yet** | No requirements | Skip, gain "Unread Letter" (persistent Memory, can retry later) |
| **Read with grandmother's perspective** | 2x "Grandmother's Hands" + 1x Gratitude | Unlock Leo's true feelings, gain "Family Love" (Warmth card) |

**Narrative Impact:**
- Read coldly → Letter confirms Leo wanted to sell studio, Maya's anger justified
- Read with pain → Letter shows Leo felt excluded, different picture emerges
- Can't read → Choice deferred, emotional state unchanged
- Grandmother's perspective → See Leo wanted Maya's approval, not money

---

### Choice Point 3: The Confrontation

**Trigger:** Maya can finally speak with Leo's echo — a manifestation of her memory of him.

**Available Approaches (determined by dominant deck family):**

**Shadow-Dominant Deck (60%+ Shadow):**
- "You abandoned us" → Argument path, Leo's echo becomes defensive
- "I did everything alone" → Victim path, Leo's echo apologizes but feels distant

**Warmth-Dominant Deck (40%+ Warmth):**
- "I miss having a brother" → Vulnerability path, Leo's echo opens up
- "Grandmother would want us to talk" → Legacy path, shared grief brings connection

**Fire-Dominant Deck (40%+ Fire):**
- "We need to settle this" → Direct path, intense but potentially productive
- "I'm angry and that's okay" → Honest path, Leo's echo mirrors honesty

**Balanced Deck (no family >40%):**
- "I don't know how to feel" → Uncertainty path, Leo's echo admits same
- "Can we start over?" → Reset path, most open-ended outcome

**Mechanic:** This is a "Confrontation" encounter type. Both Maya and Leo's echo play emotion cards. Higher emotional authenticity (matching played cards to true feelings) wins the exchange.

---

### Choice Point 4: The Truth

**Trigger:** After Confrontation, Maya can access the "True Memory" — what actually happened at the funeral.

**Prerequisites:**
- Must have watched at least 2 memory orbs
- Must have "Conflicting Memory" or "Family Love" in deck

**The Choice:**

| Option | Required Cards | Truth Revealed |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Seek the full truth** | 3 different emotion families | Grandmother asked Leo to let Maya have studio; Leo agreed but wanted to help |
| **Accept partial truth** | 2x same family | Your choice — see only what supports your current emotional state |
| **Let it go** | 2x Gratitude + 1x Acceptance (Complex) | Truth matters less than peace; skip revelation, focus on future |

**Narrative Impact:**
- Full truth → Both were trying to honor Grandmother; misunderstanding destroyed relationship
- Partial truth → Confirmation bias; emotional state reinforced
- Let it go → Mature acceptance; some questions unanswered but peace found

---

### Choice Point 5: The Resolution

**Trigger:** Final encounter. Maya must decide how to leave the Garden of Echoes.

**Available Endings (determined by deck composition and previous choices):**

#### Ending A: Reconciliation (Warmth 50%+)
**Requirements:**
- 50%+ Warmth cards in deck
- Chose "vulnerability" or "legacy" path in Confrontation
- Know the full truth

**Scene:** Maya writes to Leo. Not an apology, not a demand — an invitation to talk. She doesn't know if he'll respond, but she's opened the door.

**Final Deck Addition:** "Hopeful Heart" (Warmth/Warmth resonance card)

---

#### Ending B: Acceptance (Shadow 40%+, no Fire 30%+)
**Requirements:**
- Shadow remains significant
- Didn't let anger dominate
- Either know truth or chose "let it go"

**Scene:** Maya accepts the relationship may never heal. She keeps the studio, honors Grandmother's memory, and releases the burden of fixing what Leo broke. She's sad, but at peace.

**Final Deck Addition:** "Gentle Goodbye" (Shadow/Warmth blend — melancholy but resolved)

---

#### Ending C: Righteous Anger (Fire 50%+)
**Requirements:**
- 50%+ Fire cards
- Chose "argument" or "direct" path in Confrontation
- Never learned full truth OR rejected it

**Scene:** Maya leaves the Garden certain she was wronged. She keeps the studio, cuts Leo out completely, and channels her anger into her art. Powerful, but isolated.

**Final Deck Addition:** "Burning Certainty" (Fire/Fire resonance — powerful but prevents Warmth cards)

---

#### Ending D: Uncertain Path (Balanced, no family 40%+)
**Requirements:**
- No dominant emotion family
- Chose "uncertainty" or "reset" path in Confrontation

**Scene:** Maya leaves without a clear answer. She might reach out to Leo, she might not. The Garden taught her that feelings are complex and answers aren't always clean. She's okay with not knowing yet.

**Final Deck Addition:** "Open Question" (wild card — can count as any emotion family once per run)

## 4. Emotional Progression Arc

### Arc Structure

```
Resentment (Shadow) 
    ↓
Curiosity (Shadow → Shadow/Warmth blend)
    ↓
Conflict (Shadow/Fire blend)
    ↓
Vulnerability (Warmth emerges)
    ↓
Understanding (Warmth/Shadow blend)
    ↓
Resolution (player-chosen endpoint)
```

### How Decks Evolve

**Early Run (Encounters 1-3):**
- Mostly Shadow cards
- Limited options
- Choices tend to reinforce starting state

**Mid Run (Encounters 4-6):**
- Complex Emotions unlock
- More choice variety
- First real opportunities to shift emotional trajectory

**Late Run (Encounters 7-9):**
- Deck composition reflects player choices
- Dominant emotion family determines available endings
- Resonance combos become possible

### Meta-Progression Across Runs

Each ending unlocks new starter deck options:
- **Reconciliation ending:** Start with more Warmth cards
- **Acceptance ending:** Start with "Gentle Goodbye" (Shadow/Warmth blend)
- **Righteous Anger ending:** Start with more Fire cards
- **Uncertain Path ending:** Start with "Open Question"

**True Ending:** After experiencing all four endings, unlock "Maya's Peace" — a final run where she starts with balanced deck and can achieve complete reconciliation with full understanding.

## 5. Resolution Summary

### What Players Learn

1. **Emotions are tools, not truths** — The same situation felt through different emotions reveals different aspects
2. **Understanding requires vulnerability** — Can't reach reconciliation without risking hurt
3. **There's no "right" way to feel** — All four endings are valid, just different
4. **Perspective changes everything** — Grandmother's view, Leo's view, Maya's view — all incomplete alone

### Mechanical Takeaways

- Deck building IS character development
- Choice architecture emerges from playstyle, not just explicit decisions
- Resonance (3+ same family) represents emotional intensity — powerful but limiting
- Transmutation (combining emotions) represents growth and complexity

### Replay Value

- 4 distinct endings
- Multiple paths to each ending
- Meta-unlocks change starting conditions
- True ending requires full exploration

## 6. Design Notes

### Why This Character?

Maya represents a universal experience: family conflict where both sides have valid pain. The emotion card system lets players explore how different emotional lenses reveal different truths.

### Cozy Design Compliance

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| No failure state | All endings are valid; "Righteous Anger" isn't punished |
| No time pressure | Turn-based, contemplative pacing |
| Meaningful without punishing | Suboptimal card play still progresses story |
| Emotional safety | Player controls emotional exposure; can avoid painful memories |

### Open Questions

1. Should Leo's echo have its own "deck" in Confrontation, or is it scripted?
2. How many encounters per run? (Currently designed for 9)
3. Should we show deck composition percentage, or let players intuit it?
