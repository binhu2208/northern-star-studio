# Character 06 Concept A — Bloom

**Character Name:** Bloom  
**Emotion Family / Thematic Role:** Warmth — Joy, Hope, and Self-Worth  
**Role in Roster:** The dedicated Warmth-first character; a sustain / momentum / support playstyle that explores what happens when someone learns the difference between *performing brightness* and *actually feeling alive*.

---

## Concise Background

Bloom is someone who became "the reliable bright one" for everyone else.

They were the friend who kept the group together, the sibling who stayed cheerful during hard times, the coworker who made things easier for everyone in the room. Over time, that role stopped being generosity and started becoming survival. If Bloom was useful, kind, funny, warm enough — maybe no one would leave, maybe no one would worry, maybe they themselves would not have to look directly at how tired and unseen they had become.

They arrive in the **Garden of Echoes** not because they cannot feel joy, but because they no longer know which parts of their warmth are real and which parts are performance. Their journey is about reclaiming joy as something lived rather than displayed.

---

## 6-Phase Arc Summary

### Phase 1: Spark
*"If I keep smiling, maybe that will be enough."*

Bloom begins in reflexive positivity. Quick, bright actions. Small bursts of energy. Early cards feel helpful and optimistic, but they often prioritize momentum over honesty.

**Player feel:** Fast setup, light sustain, lots of small boosts.

### Phase 2: Radiance
*"I can hold everyone up. Just watch me."*

Their warmth grows outward, but it becomes performative and unsustainable. Bloom shines hard enough to keep others going, often at personal cost.

**Player feel:** Strong support and team-wide buffs, but prone to overextension.

### Phase 3: Glare
*"The light is too bright. I can’t keep this up."*

The mask starts to hurt. Warmth turns harsh. Cheer becomes pressure. Bloom begins punishing themselves for not being endlessly okay.

**Player feel:** High-output turns with backlash, unstable healing, risky payoff.

### Phase 4: Ashen
*"I gave everything away and still feel empty."*

Burnout. Numb kindness. The old strategies stop working. Bloom has to confront the fact that generosity without boundaries becomes self-erasure.

**Player feel:** Recovery tools, delayed payoff, rebuilding from collapse.

### Phase 5: Root
*"Maybe care can start with staying present."*

Bloom stops trying to be impressive and starts becoming grounded. Their warmth becomes quieter, steadier, and more honest.

**Player feel:** Sustainable healing, resource conversion, stronger defensive support.

### Phase 6: Bloom
*"Joy shared is stronger when it’s real."*

Integrated form. Bloom’s warmth is no longer performance or sacrifice. It is chosen, reciprocal, and alive. They can nurture themselves and others without disappearing.

**Player feel:** Stable support engine, elegant combo turns, converts care into durable momentum.

## Core Mechanics

### 1. **Glow**
Bloom’s primary resource. Built by healing, drawing, and playing Warmth cards in sequence.

- Low Glow = small sustain and setup
- High Glow = boosts support cards, empowers hybrid Warmth effects, and unlocks resonance payoffs
- If Bloom overbuilds Glow too early, some cards trigger **Glare** penalties (self-damage, discard, or weakened next turn)

**Fantasy:** Warmth can energize, but forced brightness burns out.

### 2. **Petals**
Temporary positive counters created by moments of genuine care.

Petals can be spent to:
- prevent damage
- amplify healing
- duplicate support effects
- convert excess healing into offense or card draw

**Fantasy:** Small acts of care accumulate into meaningful resilience.

### 3. **Rooted**
A stabilizing state gained in later phases.

While Rooted, Bloom gains bonuses for moderation:
- first heal each turn is stronger
- excess Glow converts into Petals instead of backlash
- ally-targeted cards also affect Bloom at reduced value

**Fantasy:** Real warmth starts with being anchored, not depleted.

### 4. **Overflow / Glare Risk**
Bloom’s unique tension.

They are not a passive healer. Their mechanic asks the player to balance generosity and self-preservation. Too much output without grounding causes backlash; grounded warmth becomes the most efficient sustain engine in the roster.

## Card Direction / Signature Playstyle

### Signature Playstyle
**Warmth combo-support with momentum sustain.**

Bloom plays like a character who turns small acts of care into scaling tempo:
- heal a little
- generate Glow
- convert Glow into Petals
- spend Petals to shield, draw, or empower the next support card
- eventually turn support into controlled offense

They should feel distinct from a generic healer:
- **not** pure stall
- **not** passive regen
- **not** naive positivity

Instead, Bloom is a **resource-cycling support engine** who rewards sequencing, pacing, and emotional balance.

### Card Direction
Likely card patterns:
- **Small Joy cards** that draw, smooth hands, or grant Petals
- **Gratitude cards** that strengthen allies, duplicate positive effects, or improve resonance
- **Contentment cards** that stabilize Glow and prevent overextension
- **Love cards** that create reciprocal effects: heal both, shield both, draw for both, cleanse both
- **Glare / burnout cards** in the mid-arc that trade huge support output for self-cost
- **Late Bloom cards** that transform excess healing into damage, card advantage, or persistent run-long value

### Mechanical Niche
If Ember is explosive output, Wren is burdened memory, Tide is tension and trust, Frost is fear management, and Maya is emotional truth through reconciliation, then Bloom is:

**the character who makes emotional regulation feel powerful.**

They reward the player for knowing when to give, when to pause, and when to let joy be enough.

## How This Character Complements the Existing 5-Character Roster

### Why Bloom belongs in the release roster

Bloom fills the cleanest missing space in the current cast:
- **Maya** uses Warmth as an earned destination, but starts from resentment and Shadow
- **Bloom** starts in Warmth and interrogates its limits
- The current roster is rich in pain-processing; Bloom adds a character focused on **renewal, reciprocity, and sustainable joy**
- Mechanically, Bloom gives the roster a true support-combo anchor without feeling like a mandatory healer

### Pairing Logic Across the Roster

#### Bloom + Maya
- Maya is about reconciliation with another person; Bloom is about not disappearing inside care for others
- Bloom gives Maya stronger Warmth support and helps stabilize Maya’s Shadow-heavy starts
- Together they create the roster’s strongest forgiveness / healing identity, but from different angles

#### Bloom + Ember
- Ember learns to use anger without destruction; Bloom learns to offer warmth without self-erasure
- Excellent Fire/Warmth tension: Ember wants to spend big, Bloom helps sustain and redirect that intensity
- Bloom can convert Ember’s self-damaging turns into recoverable tempo

#### Bloom + Wren
- Wren carries grief; Bloom creates room for life around grief
- Warmth/Shadow synergy becomes bittersweet rather than purely defensive
- Bloom helps Wren turn memory into renewal instead of only endurance

#### Bloom + Tide
- Tide fears opening up; Bloom models safe reciprocity
- Bloom’s shared buffs and mutual healing make Tide’s trust mechanics easier to trigger without removing their tension
- Warmth/Storm resonance becomes nurturing momentum instead of chaos alone

#### Bloom + Frost
- Frost acts despite fear; Bloom helps create the safety needed for that action
- Bloom’s shields, steady healing, and anti-backlash tools pair naturally with Frost’s freeze/courage cycle
- Warmth/Ice pairing gives the roster a soft but emotionally rich contrast: comfort without forcing thaw too quickly

### Roster-Level Contribution

With Bloom included, the six-character roster covers a stronger emotional spread:
- **Maya** — reconciliation / perspective / family hurt
- **Ember** — anger / consequence / responsible power
- **Wren** — grief / memory / carrying loss
- **Tide** — trust / vulnerability / connection after betrayal
- **Frost** — fear / courage / movement despite danger
- **Bloom** — joy / hope / self-worth / sustainable care

That final slot matters because it keeps Emotion Cards from becoming emotionally one-note. Bloom ensures the game is not only about surviving pain, but also about relearning how to live.

---

## Recommendation

**Bloom is a strong candidate for Character 06** because they:
- fill the missing Warmth-first role
- add a clear mechanical identity (Glow / Petals / Rooted pacing)
- complement every existing character without overlapping their core wound
- reinforce the game’s theme that integration is not just endurance — it is also renewal
