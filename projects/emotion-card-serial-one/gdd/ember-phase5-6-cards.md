# Ember Phase 5-6 Cards

**Character:** Ember  
**Emotion Family:** Fire  
**Arc:** Anger & Redemption  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Date:** March 17, 2026

---

## Overview

Ember's late-game cards represent the culmination of their emotional journey: learning to wield anger as a precise tool rather than an uncontrollable force. Where Phases 1-2 were about *intensity* and Phases 3-4 were about *consequences*, Phase 5-6 represent *mastery*.

**Design Philosophy:**
- Phase 5 (Controlled Burn) = strategic anger, efficiency over raw power
- Phase 6 (Ember) = anger as a sustainable, controlled tool — the redeemed form

---

## Phase 5: Controlled Burn

*Learning to use anger strategically. Efficiency over explosion.*

### Card 17: Bonfire

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Primary Emotion (Anger) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 7 |
| **Effect** | If you have 3+ Heat, deal +4 damage and lose no Heat |
| **Flavor** | *"Not the inferno. Just enough to keep warm."* |

**Design Notes:** The anti-Inferno. Rewards having heat without consuming it. Represents control — using the fuel without burning it up.

---

### Card 18: Forge

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Buff (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Gain 3 Heat. The next 2 Fire cards deal +3 damage. |
| **Flavor** | *"Strike while the iron's hot. But don't burn the forge."* |

**Design Notes:** Setup card that builds heat AND empowers subsequent attacks without self-damage. The "tempering" metaphor — heat shapes rather than destroys.

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Defense (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 0 |

## Phase 6: Ember

*Sustained, manageable heat. Anger as a tool — always there, never controlling.*

### Card 21: Inner Flame

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Passive (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 0 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | At the start of each turn, gain 1 Heat. Your maximum Heat is now 10. |
| **Flavor** | *"The fire never goes out. It just learns to burn steady."* |

**Design Notes:** Passive engine. Sustainable heat generation without spikes. Represents the "always there but controlled" final form. Increases max heat cap.

---

### Card 22: Phoenix Rise

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Ultimate Recovery (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 3 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Heal 8 HP. Convert all Heat into Shield (1 Heat = 1 Shield). |
| **Flavor** | *"From the ashes, I chose to rise. Different. But still burning."* |

**Design Notes:** The redemption ultimate. Transforms accumulated heat (formerly a liability) into protection. Heals AND converts heat to shield — the culmination of the arc.

---

### Card 23: Steady Heat

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Utility (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 1 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Set your Heat to exactly 4. Draw 2 cards. |
| **Flavor** | *"Not too hot. Not too cold. Just right."* |

**Design Notes:** Stability card. Resets to "sweet spot" rather than maxing or emptying. Mid-range heat is optimal for Controlled Burn synergy. Card draw enables combo plays.

---

### Card 24: Ever burning

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Finisher (Fire) |
| **Energy Cost** | 4 |
| **Damage** | 10 |
| **Effect** | If your Heat is 5-7, deal +5 damage. If 8+, deal +8 and gain 2 Energy next turn. |
| **Flavor** | *"This is who I am now. Not the fire that destroys. The fire that remains."* |

**Design Notes:** The final form finisher. Rewards having high-but-controlled heat. Bonus energy for going "hot" shows mastery — more control = more power available.

## Emotional Arc Integration

### Phase 5: Controlled Burn

- **Narrative:** Ember has survived the consequences. Now they learn to use what nearly destroyed them.
- **Mechanic:** Efficiency, sustain, using heat as a resource
- **Player Feel:** Strategic, powerful but in control

The Controlled Burn cards teach players:
1. Heat is a tool, not a liability
2. Strategic spending beats raw accumulation
3. Defense and offense can coexist (Controlled Burn, Tempered Steel)

### Phase 6: Ember

- **Narrative:** Redemption achieved. Anger is now a companion, not a conqueror.
- **Mechanic:** Sustainable engine, transformation of flaw into strength
- **Player Feel:** Masterful, balanced, complete

The Ember cards teach players:
1. Your greatest weakness becomes your greatest strength
2. Sustainability beats explosion
3. The arc is complete — from uncontrolled fire to steady flame

## Resonance & Synergy

### Heat Mechanic (Late Game)

- **3+ Heat:** +2 damage to all Fire cards (baseline from early game)
- **5+ Heat:** Controlled state — +3 damage, -1 incoming
- **8+ Heat:** Mastered state — +5 damage, +1 Energy gained

### Synergy Connections

- **Inner Flame + Ever Burning:** Passive heat fuels the finisher
- **Tempered Steel + Phoenix Rise:** Spending heat enables transformation
- **Forge + Bonfire:** Buffed attacks hit harder with heat retention
- **Steady Heat + Controlled Burn:** Maintains optimal mid-range heat

### Deck Archetype

Late-game Ember deck focuses on:
1. Maintaining 4-6 Heat (sweet spot)
2. Converting heat to other resources (shield, card draw, energy)
3. Sustainable damage over explosive bursts

---

## Balancing Notes

- Phase 5 should feel like a "second wind" — players recover from Phase 2-3 consequences
- Phase 6 should feel powerful but measured — not game-breaking, but reliable
- The heat-to-shield conversion (Phoenix Rise) is the key redemption mechanic
- Ever Burning's energy gain rewards playing "hot" responsibly

**Late-Game Deck Recommendation:**
- 1x Bonfire
- 1x Forge
- 1x Controlled Burn
- 1x Tempered Steel
- 1x Inner Flame
- 1x Phoenix Rise
- 1x Steady Heat
- 1x Ever Burning

(8 cards — sustainable, strategic, complete arc)
