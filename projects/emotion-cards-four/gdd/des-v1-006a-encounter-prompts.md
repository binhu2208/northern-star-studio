# DES-V1-006a — Encounter Prompts: Experiential Rewrites

---

## Encounter 1 — Missed Signal

**Old prompt:** "Someone important thinks they were ignored on purpose."

**New prompt:** "You meant to wave back. You did. But they were already looking away — and now the distance between you isn't physical. It's the kind where someone decides you chose to look through them. You didn't. But they don't know that yet. And every day you don't say something, it calcifies."

**Why it works:** The player is inside the moment — they feel the regret of the misread, the urgency of unsaid words, and the creeping dread that silence is making it worse. No mechanics explained, just the texture of a relationship slipping.

---

## Encounter 2 — Public Embarrassment

**Old prompt:** "A painful moment happened in front of other people, and everyone remembers it differently."

**New prompt:** "The room went quiet the wrong way. You saw it happen — the mistake, the exposure, the thing no one was supposed to see. And you saw who saw it. Now there are people walking around with a secret about you that you can't take back. Some of them feel bad. Some don't. You can feel the ones who don't."

**Why it works:** The player is trapped in the social aftermath — the weight of witnesses, the paranoia of not knowing who's holding it against them, and the heat of public shame. It puts you *in* the room, not outside it.

---

## Encounter 3 — Quiet Repair

**Old prompt:** "After the damage, there is one private chance to see whether anything can still be rebuilt."

**New prompt:** "They're sitting across from you, not saying much. The easy stuff is gone. What you're both sitting in now is the hard part — the part where one of you has to go first and mean it. You can see them deciding whether to bother. The question isn't whether you want this. It's whether you're both willing to feel uncomfortable to get it back."

**Why it works:** Fragile and uncertain — the player feels the risk of the conversation, the possibility that this could go either way, and the uncomfortable truth that repair requires going through the hard part, not around it.

## Encounter 4 — Old Grudge

**Old prompt:** *(new, not yet in data.js)*

**New prompt:** "They brought it up. Of course they brought it up. You thought you were past it — maybe they thought so too, or maybe they just never were. Now you're being held accountable for who you were, not who you are. You can't argue your way out of the past. You can only show them what you're willing to carry forward."

**Why it works:** The player feels the trap of being defined by their past self, the frustration of not being able to rewrite history, and the painful reality that someone else's wounds don't care how much you've grown. The encounter is about carrying old weight into new ground.

---

## Encounter 5 — Breakthrough Moment

**Old prompt:** *(new, not yet in data.js)*

**New prompt:** "Everything you've been through in this run led here. The ones you hurt, the ones you didn't fix, the ones you almost lost and didn't know it until later. You can feel all of it at once — and for once, it's not heavy. It's clear. This is the moment where you either say what you actually mean, or you let the opportunity pass and carry the silence instead."

**Why it works:** The player feels the accumulated weight of the run behind them and the singular, trembling possibility of what could be on the other side. It's the climax because it's the first time everything is in the open — and the player has to decide what to do with that.
