# Emotion Cards Four — ART-002 Frame Roughs + Typography Pairing

## Purpose
Concise style exploration focused on **prototype-readable card frames** and **type pairings** for the 5 card families defined in ART-001. Direction stays template-first, stream-readable, and safe for tuning.

## Direction Summary
Recommended overall visual lane: **soft drama, editorial UI, emotionally legible without fantasy over-rendering**.

Working principles pulled from project docs:
- category recognition must beat decoration,
- one clear primary effect + one optional conditional,
- text stays data-driven and flexible,
- breakthrough should read as earned payoff, not normal card traffic,
- spectator readability matters as much as player readability.

---

## Frame Rough Directions by Family

### 1. Emotion
**Read:** immediate tone / stance / feeling brought into the scene.

**Rough direction:**
- rounded upper frame shoulders,
- broad color band at top edge,
- soft inner glow behind illustration slot,
- category icon near title for quick hand read,
- body panel slightly open and airy.

**Visual cues:** warm-to-heavy gradients, breathable spacing, less rigid corners.

**Why it fits:** Emotion cards are the clearest “how am I entering this moment?” cards, so the frame should feel open and expressive first.

### 2. Memory
**Read:** context, shared history, emotional baggage, callback.

**Rough direction:**
- inset frame with layered border,
- subtle archival/notebook divider line,
- slightly narrower illustration window,
- small secondary band or tab for support-role feel,
- cue line or bracket motif suggesting recall/annotation.

**Visual cues:** faded warmth, quieter contrast, slightly more texture than other families.

**Why it fits:** Memory should feel contextual and supportive, not loud. The frame can imply depth/history without becoming vintage cosplay.

### 3. Reaction
**Read:** immediate response, coping move, defense, de-escalation, pressure.

**Rough direction:**
- sharper angles and stronger edge contrast,
- more active side rails,
- title band feels tighter and more compressed,
- effect area slightly more prominent than art slot,
- state-change markers sit closer to the center to stress action.

**Visual cues:** brisk diagonals, firmer corners, high-contrast badges.

**Why it fits:** Reaction cards are the “what happens now” family. The frame should look faster and more tactical.

### 4. Shift
**Read:** reframe, pivot, reinterpretation, change of lens.

**Rough direction:**
- split-frame or offset-panel construction,
- one interrupted line crossing the frame to imply change,
- icon container can break normal alignment slightly,
- illustration slot may use a subtle dual-plane or overlaid shape,
- support text area gets strong separation for condition clarity.

**Visual cues:** transitional geometry, asymmetric accents, controlled contrast flips.

**Why it fits:** Shift cards should visibly suggest that the encounter state is being re-read rather than merely pushed harder.

### 5. Breakthrough
**Read:** earned outcome / resolution threshold / payoff state.

**Rough direction:**
- distinct halo or crown shape around title,
- strongest value contrast of any family,
- more generous margins and cleaner frame silhouette,
- result banner zone integrated into lower frame,
- limited highlight foil-look simulated through light bands, not busy ornament.

**Visual cues:** luminous center, stable geometry, celebratory but restrained finish.

**Why it fits:** Breakthrough must feel special on sight, but still live inside the same prototype system.

---

## Shared Frame System Notes
Across all 5 families, keep these constants:
- same card proportions,
- same title position,
- same effect text block logic,
- same stat/keyword chip behavior,
- same illustration slot footprint,
- category distinction driven by top band, corner language, icon housing, and accent geometry.

This supports fast iteration and clean hand readability.

---

## Typography Pairing Recommendations

### Pairing A — Best prototype default
**Titles / labels:** Inter Tight SemiBold  
**Body / rules text:** Atkinson Hyperlegible Regular / Bold

**Why:**
- excellent UI readability on small cards,
- slightly condensed title voice without getting theatrical,
- body copy stays very legible for streams and quick scanning,
- easy to ship in prototype workflows.

**Best use:** safest all-around choice for first playable slice.

### Pairing B — Slightly more editorial / emotional
**Titles / labels:** Fraunces SemiBold  
**Body / rules text:** Inter Regular / Medium

**Why:**
- gives card names a little more personality and emotional tone,
- keeps rules text clean and digital underneath,
- useful if the prototype needs more identity in screenshots.

**Caution:** use sparingly at small sizes; Fraunces should stay in titles only.

---

## Recommendation
If only one direction moves forward now:
- use **Emotion = soft rounded**, **Memory = inset archival**, **Reaction = angular active**, **Shift = offset transitional**, **Breakthrough = luminous resolved**,
- adopt **Inter Tight + Atkinson Hyperlegible** as the first readability pairing.

This gives ART-002 a clean prototype lane and leaves room for ART-004 to formalize icon and component rules.
