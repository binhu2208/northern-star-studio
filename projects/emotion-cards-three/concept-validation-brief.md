# Emotion Cards Three — Concept Validation / Prototype Brief

## 1. Purpose
Validate whether Emotion Cards Three has a strong enough core hook to justify vertical-slice investment.

This brief is designed to answer one question:
**Can the team prove that “emotions as strategy” is tactically meaningful, visually readable, and marketable in a short prototype?**

## 2. Prototype Goal
Deliver a small, playable prototype that demonstrates:
- one clear emotional-state gameplay loop
- one readable combat scenario
- one screenshot-worthy visual state
- one short clip-worthy combat beat
- one first-session experience that players can understand quickly

This is **not** a production build and **not** a content-scale milestone. It is a validation milestone.

## 3. Scope Boundaries
### In scope
- one playable combat scenario
- one emotional-state model
- one starter deck / archetype path
- placeholder UI sufficient for readability testing
- enough presentation quality to evaluate screenshot and clip potential
- first-session onboarding/readability checks

### Out of scope
- multiple polished characters
- long-form campaign structure
- broad content pool
- final art production
- platform porting
- monetization or live-service planning

## 4. Core Validation Questions
1. **Tactical meaning**
   - Does the emotional-state system create real strategic decisions?
   - Can players recognize why changing an emotional state matters?

2. **Readability**
   - Can players quickly read emotional states, card impact, and likely outcomes?
   - Can the prototype generate at least one screenshot that is understandable and attractive?

3. **Marketability**
   - Can the game be pitched clearly in one sentence?
   - Can the prototype produce one short combat clip that communicates the hook?

4. **First-session comprehension**
   - Can a new player understand the prototype quickly enough to stay engaged?
   - Do they understand what the emotional mechanic is doing within the first session?

## 5. Success Criteria
The prototype is considered successful if it proves all of the following:

### A. Gameplay success
- at least one emotion loop produces tactically meaningful decisions
- players can explain why a specific emotional state affected a combat choice
- the loop feels distinct from a generic deckbuilder modifier system

### B. Readability success
- testers can identify the current emotional state without confusion
- testers can infer likely gameplay impact from visual/state cues
- at least one screenshot is considered “store-legible” by team review

### C. UX success
- first-session players understand the basic loop quickly
- onboarding friction is low enough that confusion does not dominate feedback
- card/state interactions remain readable during active combat

### D. Marketing success
- one-line pitch remains clear and compelling after prototype review
- one short combat clip can show the hook without extra explanation
- the prototype supports a believable wishlist-facing pitch direction

## 6. Failure Criteria / Red Flags
The prototype should be considered at risk if:
- emotional states feel cosmetic or interchangeable
- testers cannot explain the strategic value of the emotion system
- UI/readability problems prevent understanding
- the best screenshot still looks generic or confusing
- the hook requires too much explanation to feel interesting
- the scope required to “make it work” starts expanding too quickly

## 7. Deliverables
This prototype phase should produce:
- one playable prototype scenario
- one documented emotional-state model
- one readability guide / screenshot legibility checklist
- one first-session QA/usability checklist
- one screenshot candidate
- one short combat clip candidate
- one greenlight recommendation package

## 9. Recommended Review Gate
At the end of prototype validation, the team should answer:
- Did the emotional mechanic create meaningful play?
- Did the prototype produce one readable screenshot?
- Did the prototype produce one readable short clip?
- Did first-session players understand what was happening?
- Is the concept different enough to justify the next milestone?

If the answer is **no** to any major question, the team should revise the concept before greenlighting a vertical slice.

## 10. Recommendation
Proceed with a **small, disciplined prototype** focused on proving the hook — not proving scale.

The win condition is not “we built a lot.”
The win condition is:
**we proved this game is strategically distinct, visually readable, and marketable.**
