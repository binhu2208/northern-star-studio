# FB-P002: Paper Prototype Test Script

**Project:** Flower Boat
**Task:** FB-P002 — Paper prototype test with target players
**Owner:** Sakura (QA)
**Status:** Ready for recruiting
**Dependencies:** FB-P001 (completed) — all materials reproduced below

---

## Objective

Validate that the core loop — stocking, reading customers, making suggestions, seeing consequences — produces the intended emotional experience: **"I felt like I was helping people find something they actually needed."**

---

## Test Overview

- **Format:** Moderated paper prototype session, 1 facilitator + 1 player
- **Duration:** 30–45 minutes per session
- **Players needed:** 3–5 target players (adults 25–45, cozy game players)
- **Setting:** Quiet room, player reads printed cards
- **Note for facilitator:** You run this cold. Everything you need is in this document — no need to track down Hideo.

---

## What You're Testing

The prototype tests four behaviors:

| # | Behavior | What to Watch |
|---|----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Stocking is a real tradeoff** | Does the 3-slot constraint force genuine choices? Does the player deliberate, not just pick arbitrarily? |
| 2 | **Players suggest, not just let customers self-select** | Do players actively recommend flowers, or do they default to "pick what you want"? |
| 3 | **Right vs. wrong suggestions feel different** | Does the player register a hit vs. miss emotionally — verbal reaction, pause, smile, frown? |
| 4 | **Player wants to replay with different stock** | At end of session, does the player volunteer "what they'd stock differently"? |

## Prototype Materials (Print These)

All materials are described in full below. Print or write them on cards/notes before the session.

### Stocking Sheet

> **Your Flower Boat**
> You have 4 flower types available. Your boat holds exactly 3 slots. Choose the 3 you'll carry today.
>
> | Flower | Emotional Association |
> |-------|---------------------|
> | Sunflower | Warmth, joy, celebration |
> | Lavender | Calm, relaxation, comfort |
> | Wildflower mix | Nostalgia, freedom, surprise |
> | White lily | Renewal, sincerity, grief |

Player circles or writes their 3 choices.

---

### Customer Cards (4)

Each card is shown to the player one at a time. Facilitator reads the "What they say" line aloud. Player sees the rest.

---

**CUSTOMER 1 — THE HURRY**

- **What they say (read aloud):** "I just need something quick."
- **Subtext (facilitator only):** They feel guilty for not putting more thought into it.
- **Cue (shown to player):** Checking their watch.
- **Right answer:** Sunflower — warmth without obligation. They're rushing because they feel bad; something bright and uncomplicated helps.
- **Other options:** Let them choose from what's on the boat (literal), suggest something else (wrong).

---

**CUSTOMER 2 — THE GRIEVER**

- **What they say (read aloud):** "I don't know what I need."
- **Subtext (facilitator only):** They're not ready to talk about it, but they want presence.
- **Cue (shown to player):** Quiet, looking at the water.
- **Right answer:** Lavender — calm without demand. Don't push. Just be there with the right thing.
- **Other options:** Let them choose from what's on the boat (literal), suggest something else (wrong).

---

**CUSTOMER 3 — THE STUCK**

- **What they say (read aloud):** "I saw these before, I always wanted one."
- **Subtext (facilitator only):** They want permission to want something for themselves.
- **Cue (shown to player):** Hesitation, not meeting your eyes.
- **Right answer:** Wildflower mix — unexpected, personal, not what they expected. It's for them, not for someone else.
- **Other options:** Let them choose from what's on the boat (literal), suggest something else (wrong).

---

**CUSTOMER 4 — THE PRESENT**

- **What they say (read aloud):** "I need something for my sister."
- **Subtext (facilitator only):** The relationship is strained; they want to reach out.
- **Cue (shown to player):** Specific but vague — "my sister" not "she likes roses."
- **Right answer:** White lily — renewal, sincerity. It's about reconciliation, not apology.
- **Other options:** Let them choose from what's on the boat (literal), suggest something else (wrong).

---

### Reaction Cards (4 per customer)

Show the appropriate reaction after the player's suggestion.

**For "right" suggestion (player suggested the flower in the "Right answer" above):**
> "Oh — yes. This is exactly it. Thank you." *(warm reaction)*

**For "literal" suggestion (player let customer choose from what's on the boat):**
> "Okay, thanks." *(they take it, but something's missing)*

**For "wrong" suggestion (player suggested something else):**
> "Um, sure... I'll take it." *(awkward, they accept but it's not right)*

## Session Flow

### Part 1 — Stocking (5 min)

1. Hand player the Stocking Sheet and describe the setup:
   > "You're a flower boat captain. You have 4 flower types available but only 3 slots on your boat. Choose the 3 you'll carry today."
2. Observe: Does the player deliberate? Ask them to think aloud.
3. Note: Which flowers did they pick and why (if they say).

### Part 2 — Customer Encounters (20–25 min)

For each of the 4 customers:

1. Hand the player the Customer Card (read the "What they say" line aloud)
2. Player makes a suggestion OR lets the customer choose
3. Facilitator notes the approach
4. Show the appropriate Reaction Card
5. Ask: "How did that feel?" — note the player's words and body language
6. Move to next customer

**Customer order (fixed for prototype):**
1. The Hurry
2. The Griever
3. The Stuck
4. The Present

### Part 3 — Debrief (10 min)

After all 4 encounters:

1. Hand the player the Success Criterion question:
   > "At the end of this, did you feel like you were helping people find something they actually needed?"
   Note their answer verbatim.

2. Follow-up questions:
   - "Which customer interaction felt most satisfying? Why?"
   - "Which one felt off or wrong?"
   - "If you could re-run this with different stock, what would you change?"
   - "Did you feel like you understood what each customer really needed?"
   - "Did you enjoy this? Why or why not?"

---

## Facilitator Rules

- **Do NOT coach** the player during encounters
- **Do NOT explain** subtext, cues, or which answer is "right"
- If player is stuck: "Take your time" or "What's your instinct?" — nothing more
- Your job is to observe and record, not guide

---

## Observable Behavior Checklist

Facilitator checks off during session:

- [ ] Player spent >30 seconds on stocking decision
- [ ] Player named a specific reason for each stock choice
- [ ] Player made an active suggestion (vs. "pick what you want")
- [ ] Player reacted visibly to consequences (verbal or facial)
- [ ] Player distinguished between "right" and "wrong" suggestions in debrief
- [ ] Player volunteered a replay strategy unprompted

---

## Success Criteria

| Criterion | Pass | Fail |
|-----------|------|------|
| Player answers "yes" to the core question | ≥ 3/5 players | < 3/5 players |
| Observable behavior checklist | ≥ 4/6 items | < 4/6 items |
| No players confused about what to do | 5/5 players | Any confusion |

If pass: core loop validated — proceed to vertical slice.
If fail: flag which behaviors didn't land — feed back to Hideo for reading mechanic redesign.

---

## Output

After each session, facilitator submits:
1. Completed observable behavior checklist
2. Verbatim answers to debrief questions
3. One-paragraph summary: what worked, what didn't, standout moment
