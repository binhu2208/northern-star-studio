# Flower Boat — Paper Prototype

## Project
Flower Boat

## Task ID
FB-P001

## Purpose
Test whether the core loop (stock the boat, read customers, suggest flowers, see consequences) creates the feeling of helping people find something they actually needed.

## What to Test
1. Does the stock constraint (3 slots from 4 flowers) force meaningful choices?
2. Are customer subtext and cues readable enough to guide suggestions?
3. Does the player feel the difference between a right and wrong suggestion?
4. Does the player want to play again with different stock?

## What You Need

### Printouts
- 1 × Route Map
- 4 × Customer Cards
- 4 × Flower Cards
- 1 × Stock Selection Sheet

## Customer Cards

### Customer 1 — The Hurry

**Name:** (just a description)
**Type:** The Hurry
**What they say:** "I just need something quick. I'm running late."

**Subtext:** They feel guilty for not putting more thought into it. They want to do something nice but don't have bandwidth.

**Cue:** Checking their watch. Standing on one foot. Eyes already on the next thing.

**What they actually need:** Something warm and uncomplicated. Sunflower — warmth without obligation. Bright enough to feel like a real gift, simple enough not to demand anything back.

---

### Customer 2 — The Griever

**Name:** (just a description)
**Type:** The Griever
**What they say:** "I don't know what I need. I just... needed to get out."

**Subtext:** They're not ready to talk about it, but they want presence. They're not looking for conversation — they're looking for something to hold onto.

**Cue:** Quiet. Looking at the water. Not making eye contact. Standing still in a way that feels heavy.

**What they actually need:** Lavender — calm without demand. Not trying to fix anything, just... a little softness. Something that says "I see you" without asking anything.

---

### Customer 3 — The Stuck

**Name:** (just a description)
**Type:** The Stuck
**What they say:** "I saw these before, I always wanted one. But I don't know which."

**Subtext:** They want permission to want something for themselves. They've been putting themselves last. This is a small act of self-care.

**Cue:** Hesitation. Not meeting your eyes. Starting sentences and stopping. Touching things without buying.

**What they actually need:** Wildflower mix — unexpected, personal, not what they expected. It's not for someone else. It's for them. That's the whole point.

---

### Customer 4 — The Present

**Name:** (just a description)
**Type:** The Present
**What they say:** "I need something for my sister. She's... going through a hard time."

**Subtext:** The relationship is strained. They want to reach out but don't know how. This flower is a gesture, not a solution.

**Cue:** Specific but vague — "my sister" not "she likes roses." Weight in their voice when they say "hard time."

**What they actually need:** White lily — renewal, sincerity, grief. It's about reconciliation, not apology. It's not saying sorry. It's saying "I still care about you."

## How to Run the Test

1. **Explain the game** — "You're a flower boat delivery person. You stock your boat with 3 types of flowers, visit 4 customers, and try to help them find the right flower."
2. **Hand over the flower cards** — Let them read the emotional associations
3. **Let them choose stock** — Ask them to fill in the Stock Selection Sheet
4. **Run through stops 1-4** — Read each customer card aloud. Let them decide. Record the result.
5. **Ask the reflection questions** — At the end, ask the Post-Play Reflection questions

**Watch for:**
- Do they read the subtext before deciding?
- Do they think about stock before or after seeing customers?
- Do they notice when they got it right vs. wrong?
- Do they want to play again?

**Note what they say, not just what they choose.**
