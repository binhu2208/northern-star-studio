# Flower Boat — Market Brief

## One-Line Hook
A meditative canal-cruising flower delivery game where thoughtful inventory and route choices turn everyday errands into small acts of care.

---

## Target Audience
- **Primary:** Adults 25–45, skewing female, who play cozy games (Animal Crossing, Stardew, Unpacking) and seek low-stress, high-meaning gameplay
- **Secondary:** Stress-relief seekers — burnout recovery, anxiety management — who want a calm digital hobby
- **Tertiary:** Accessibility-curious players drawn by "cozy" branding but underserved by fast-paced indie market

---

## Market Positioning
**"The meditative commute"** — a cozy game built around the ritual of getting somewhere, not just optimizing output.

The cozy game market is maturing. Players who grew up on Animal Crossing are aging into grounded, mundane-life sims. Flower Boat slots into the growing "quiet games" subgenre (Gonkys, A Little to the Left, Untitled Goose Game's vibe without the chaos) with a specific aesthetic: canal Venice/Amsterdam, seasonal weather shifts, soft watercolor palette.

The delivery-loop mechanic gives structure without stakes — a proven pattern applied to a low-pressure context.

---

## Differentiation
1. **Thematic specificity** — No other cozy game centers on floristry + boating. Strong visual identity (canal + flowers) is instantly distinctive on store shelves.
2. **Influence loop** — Most cozy games are decoration or farming. Flower Boat adds a light systems layer: you shape what customers want through presentation and timing. Low-complexity, high-feeling of agency.
3. **Weather × time-of-day as aesthetic, not obstacle** — No punishing weather; just different moods and inventory opportunities. Captures the *feeling* of a commute changing over time.
4. **Scale ceiling** — Seasonal content drops (spring tulips, winter wreaths), new canal districts, customer relationship trees. Low dev cost per expansion.

## Risk Profile

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Genre ceiling — cozy needs strong TikTok/IG momentum to breakout | Moderate | Start building audience early via devlogs and concept art |
| "Influencing customers" drifts into complex economics | Low | Stay light — one inventory choice per stop, no economy sim |
| Mobile port undermines intentionality | Low | PC/console-first unless specific mobile demand surfaces |
| Base loop feels thin without seasonal content | Low-Moderate | Nail the 10-minute session feel first; content is secondary |

**Overall: Low-to-moderate.** Cozy game production costs are manageable, and the niche is underserved at the "mundane logistics" intersection.

---

## Near-Term Validation Approach

**Week 1–2 — Concept test:**
3–5 screenshots + one short gameplay GIF. Post to r/cozygaming and one indie dev Discord. Measure save/interest ratio. No build required.

**Month 1 — Paper prototype:**
A simple inventory × customer matrix in a spreadsheet. Playtest with 5–8 target players. Does "choosing what flowers to stock" feel meaningful or arbitrary?

**Month 2–3 — Vertical slice:**
One canal district, 3 weather states, one customer type, 8–10 flowers. Prove the 10-minute loop feels complete. Post devlog on TikTok/IG. Measure watch-through and comment sentiment.

**Validation signal:** If concept art gets saved/shared organically vs. other devlog content — that's a strong green light. Cozy audiences respond to aesthetic resonance before mechanics.

---

## Recommendation
**Proceed to paper prototype.** The niche is real, the thematic angle is defensible, and the production scope is appropriate for a small studio. Validate the influence loop first — if "choosing inventory shapes customer outcomes" doesn't land, the game is just a boat tour. If it does, you have a franchise seed.
