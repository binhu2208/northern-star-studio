## Overview

Ember's Phase 5 represents the midpoint of their emotional arc: learning to wield anger as a precise tool rather than an uncontrollable force. This is **mastered fire** — heat contained, shaped, and made useful. The visual language should communicate **discipline, efficiency, and controlled intensity** rather than chaotic destruction or triumphant rebirth.

This document consolidates:
- Art asset requirements for Phase 5
- Card-by-card art specifications
- Character portrait progression notes
- Visual style reference points

## Card Art Specifications

### Shared Visual Language — Phase 5 / Controlled Burn

All four cards share these visual rules to create batch coherence:

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Core Read** | Controlled intensity, not chaos |
| **Shape Language** | Contained flame shapes, forged arcs, circular heat halos, cleaner silhouettes than Phases 1-2 |
| **Composition** | Central or clearly anchored focal point; less explosion, more directed flow |
| **Value Structure** | Strong focal contrast, simpler background masses, avoid noisy ember scatter |
| **Palette** | Mature orange-red fire with brass, iron, soot black, tempered gold accents; white-hot highlights sparingly |
| **Lighting** | Directional firelight with stable shadows; sustained glow, not strobe-like flare |
| **Environment** | Hearth, forge, ash, metal, controlled firebreaks — not open disaster |
| **Readability** | Each card reads at card size in under 1 second |

---

### Card 17 — Bonfire

**Emotional Read:** Warm strength. Anger has become steady fuel — protective, communal, alive, but not dangerous.

**Focal Point:** Contained bonfire core burning bright and stable, with Ember tending it as calm keeper.

**Composition:** Medium shot with bonfire centered low-mid frame. Flame mass compact and upward. Ember can be seated, crouched, or standing nearby in control. Use darkness or cool negative space to make fire feel intentional.

**Palette:** Warm oranges, amber, softer gold. Light feels comforting, not violent. Hottest whites limited to fire core. Background: deep charcoal or muted dusk.

**Symbolism:** "Just enough" heat — survival, steadiness, chosen warmth. The anti-inferno.

**Crop Notes:** Fire shape must survive 3:2 crop cleanly. Ember silhouette secondary if included.

---

### Card 18 — Forge

**Emotional Read:** Focused preparation. Power is being shaped, not unleashed.

**Focal Point:** Strike moment at the forge — Ember shaping heated metal, or white-hot workpiece on anvil.

**Composition:** Strong diagonal from hammer/arm motion into glowing metal. Tight or medium-close. Sparks follow action arc, not random spray.

**Palette:** Hot iron — orange, yellow-white highlight, blackened steel, deep red heat bloom. Hard local light from metal and forge bed. Background subdued.

**Symbolism:** Heat as a tool for making something stronger. Forge = discipline, timing, constructive anger.

**Crop Notes:** Hammer/workpiece interaction inside safe center. If Ember visible, glowing strike moment does the work at small size.

---

### Card 19 — Controlled Burn

**Emotional Read:** Calm regulation under pressure. Ember sustaining heat without being consumed.

**Focal Point:** Contained line or ring of fire under Ember's command, with Ember protected inside or precisely guiding the burn.

**Composition:** Strong enclosed shape: circular firebreak, measured burn line, or low sweeping arc. Ember composed and upright, not bracing in panic. Cleaner, more graphic than other cards.

**Palette:** Controlled orange-red flame, darker ground, ash gray, restrained smoke. Less brightness than Bonfire/Forge. Efficient, not spectacular. Subtle shield-like glow can support defensive mechanic.

**Symbolism:** Fire being tended, bounded, repurposed. Anger acknowledged, maintained, kept useful.

**Crop Notes:** Containment shape remains legible in 600×400. Prioritize one clean fire boundary.

---

### Card 20 — Tempered Steel

**Emotional Read:** Hardened resilience. Strength gained through endured heat and deliberate cooling.

**Focal Point:** Forged steel object or defensive silhouette — blade, plate, shield, or abstract metal barrier — with Ember's fire visibly having tempered it.

**Composition:** Close-up or medium-close with steel form dominating. Transition from heat to protection: lingering glow in metal, cooling edges, Ember's hand withdrawing. Solid, defensive, not explosive.

**Palette:** Steel blue-gray, iron black, ember orange, restrained gold edge light. Hot interior glow against cool hardened surface. Least flame-dominant card in batch.

**Symbolism:** "Forged, not broken." Heat creates armor, resolve, structure — not just destruction.

**Crop Notes:** Bold steel silhouette reads instantly. If shield/blade motif, keep main form large enough for UI overlays.

## Character Portrait Progression

Ember's character portraits track their emotional journey from uncontrolled rage to mastered control:

| Phase | Portrait Name | Emotional State | Visual Notes |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|---------------|

| 2 | blaze | Chaotic intensity, overwhelming | Tense pose, highest contrast, hair wild but directionally focused |

For Phase 5, we need **1 portrait variant**:

| Portrait | Expression | Pose | Notes |

**Controlled (Phase 5):**
- Brows: Neutral to slightly focused, not furrowed
- Eyes: Direct gaze, calm intensity — "I know what I'm doing"
- Mouth: Neutral or subtle firm line — not smiling, not grimacing
- Overall: Someone who has earned their composure

## Visual Style Reference Points

### Phase 5 Mood Keywords
- Banked coals
- Forge glow  
- Tempered steel
- Contained heat
- Disciplined intensity
- Hearth fire, not wildfire

### Material / Surface Cues
- Oxidized metal with blue-brown heat temper colors
- Sooted stone and firebrick with ash deposits
- Charred wood — blackened exterior, dim red life within
- Hammered steel catching small bands of hot reflection
- Ash, clinker, coal dust as grounding textures

### Lighting Direction
- Localized heat sources: forge mouths, coal beds, brazier bowls
- Deep surrounding shadow for containment feel
- Dense, selective highlights — hot whites only at hottest points
- Practical, weighty firelight — almost industrial, not whimsical

### What to Avoid
- Giant background fire spectacle swallowing the frame
- Soaring silhouettes, wing-like flame shapes → drifts toward Phase 6
- Sparks/smoke filling every edge → weakens control feel
- Generic red-orange fantasy fire gradients without material context
- Screaming faces or explosive action beats → Phase 5 is mastery, not rage
