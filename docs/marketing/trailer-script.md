# Emotion Cards — Trailer Script & Storyboard

**Project:** Emotion Cards  
**Document:** Trailer Script  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** March 17, 2026  
**Author:** Gabe 📊

---

## Trailer Concept

**Duration:** 45 seconds (optimal range: 30-60 seconds)  
**Tone:** Emotionally evocative, warm, slightly mysterious — like peeking into someone else's memory.  
**Target Audience:** Indie game enthusiasts, deckbuilder fans, narrative game lovers, cozy game players  

**Logline:** *"Feel your way through. Every card is an emotion. Every choice is a step toward understanding."*

---

## Music & Mood Suggestions

### Primary Track
- **Vibe:** Cinematic indie-folk with electronic textures — like Ólafur Arnalds meets Kento.
- **Temp:** Starts quiet/minimal (45 BPM), builds through the gameplay section (90-100 BPM), lands warm/hopeful at CTA.
- **References:** "In the Morning" by Yann Tiersen, "Postcard Faint" by Hovet, "River Flows in You" (but less cliché).

### Sound Design Layer
- Soft card shuffles and paper textures
- Gentle ambient hum (emotional resonance)
- Muted piano chimes on card plays
- Rising swell during emotional moments

---

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### SCENE 1: THE HOOK (0:00-0:08)

**Visual:** Close-up of hands holding a worn, glowing card. The card shows a simple illustration — a single ray of light through clouds. Text fades in: *"What if your feelings were your greatest weapon?"*

**Audio:** Near silence. Just breathing. Then — a single, resonant piano note.

**Mood:** Intimate, curious, slightly mysterious.

---

### SCENE 2: THE WORLD REVEAL (0:08-0:15)

**Visual:** Pull back to show a vast, ethereal library/mind-scape. Floating shelves of glowing cards. Camera glides through as cards drift past — each one pulsing with color:
- Warm amber (Joy)
- Cool blue (Sadness)  
- Flickering red (Anger)
- Misty purple (Fear)

**Voiceover (soft, introspective):** *"In Emotion Cards, your deck isn't just a tool — it's your emotional state."*

**Audio:** Music begins to build gently. Card sounds — soft clinks, whooshes.

**Mood:** Wonder, discovery, vast possibility.

---

### SCENE 3: GAMEPLAY — CARD MECHANICS (0:15-0:25)

**Visual:** Fast montage of gameplay footage:
- Player plays "Twinge of Hope" (Joy) → card glows, character smiles
- Player chains 3 Fire cards → screen ignites, "RESONANCE" text flashes
- Opposing families collide (Fire + Water) → cards scatter, "TURBULENCE" ripples
- A card transforms: Anger + Time → becomes "Understanding"

**Voiceover:** *"Build your emotional state. Chain feelings together. Let them transform."*

**Audio:** Music peaks — hopeful, driving. Card sounds layer: shuffles, plays, clashes, transforms.

**Mood:** Exciting, strategic, satisfying.

---

### SCENE 4: NARRATIVE HEART (0:25-0:35)

**Visual:** Character-focused moment. We see an NPC — an older woman, face lined with years. Player chooses to play a Sadness card. The woman's expression softens. A memory bubble opens, showing a faded photograph.

**Voiceover (warm):** *"Every conversation is a chance to understand. Every choice reshapes who you become."*

Cut to: Different run. Same NPC. Player plays Gratitude instead. The woman laughs, bright and warm. The memory bubble shows a celebration, colors vivid.

**Audio:** Music softens, becomes tender. No card sounds — just ambient warmth.

**Mood:** Moving, meaningful, human.

---

### SCENE 5: PROGRESSION & POSSIBILITY (0:35-0:42)

**Visual:** Montage of progression:
- New cards unlocked — beautiful, unique designs
- Relationship map illuminates as paths complete
- Multiple character arcs branching and connecting
- A "true ending" moment — two characters embracing, surrounded by every emotion card in the game, all glowing in harmony

**Voiceover:** *"No failure. Only growth. No endings — only deeper understanding."*

**Audio:** Music swells to full crescendo, then settles into warmth.

**Mood:** Accomplishment, hope, invitation.

---

### SCENE 6: CALL TO ACTION (0:42-0:45)

**Visual:** Game logo appears — elegant, minimalist. Below it:

> **WISHLIST ON STEAM**

> *"Coming Soon"*

Small text: *Wishlist now for exclusive starter deck: "First Light"*

**Audio:** Final note rings out — hopeful, open-ended. Slight pause. Then silence.

**Mood:** Confident, inviting.

## Key Visuals to Capture

1. **Card Art Quality** — Show that each emotion card is beautifully illustrated, distinct
2. **Resonance Effects** — Visual payoff for smart plays (glowing chains)
3. **Character Emotion** — NPCs must feel real, expressive
4. **UI Warmth** — Cozy, safe, inviting interface
5. **Transformation** — The Anger → Understanding moment is crucial
