## Target Player Segments

### Primary Segment A: Deckbuilder/Roguelite Veterans
**Profile:** Players with 100+ hours in Slay the Spire, Monster Train, Hades
- **What they want:** Strategic depth, replayability, build optimization
- **Hook for Emotion Cards:** Emotions add new strategic layer; narrative consequence gives runs meaning beyond high scores
- **Key message:** "Mechanically deep deckbuilder where your emotional choices actually matter"

### Primary Segment B: Narrative Indie Seekers
**Profile:** Players of Disco Elysium, Pentiment, Citizen Sleeper, Sea of Stars
- **What they want:** Story weight, meaningful choices, character-driven experience
- **Hook for Emotion Cards:** First deckbuilder where emotional identity shapes story outcomes
- **Key message:** "A deckbuilder with the narrative consequence of a story RPG"

### Secondary Segment C: Cozy Strategy Players
**Profile:** Players of Balatro, Dorfromantik, Mini Metro, Slay the Spire (chill runs)
- **What they want:** Satisfying gameplay without aggression, beautiful presentation
- **Hook for Emotion Cards:** Warmth family mechanics, no failure state, emotionally safe design
- **Key message:** "Strategic depth with heart — no punishment, just perspective shifts"

### Tertiary Segment D: Content Creators
**Profile:** YouTubers/streamers covering deckbuilders, indie games, roguelites
- **What they want:** Fresh hook, shareable moments, discoverable games to cover
- **Hook for Emotion Cards:** Unique emotional mechanics = great reaction content; runsplaining narrative cards = engaging streams

## Notable Risks & Opportunities

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |

| **"Another deckbuilder" fatigue** | HIGH | HIGH | Lead every asset with emotional-strategy hook, NOT "deckbuilder" alone |
| **Slay the Spire 2 competition** (released March 2026) | HIGH | MEDIUM | Position as "the emotional alternative" — different audience, not head-to-head |
| **Messaging drift across channels** | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Lock one core pitch; reuse everywhere: "narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions shape strategy" |
| **Wishlist → purchase drop-off** | MEDIUM | HIGH | Shift decisively from "wishlist" to "out now" at launch; use hard CTAs |
| **Tone confusion** (cozy vs. strategy) | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Pair visuals with gameplay clarity; pair mechanics with emotional framing |

| Opportunity | Likelihood | Impact | Why Now |
