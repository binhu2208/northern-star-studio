# Emotion Cards — Launch Brief

**Project:** Emotion Cards  
**Owner:** Gabe 📊  
**Branch Target:** `release/v0.5.0`  
**Status:** Ready for execution

## Launch Objective

Launch **Emotion Cards** with a clear, repeatable pitch that converts existing interest into:
- launch-week Steam purchases
- early player reviews and social proof
- creator and press coverage that extends beyond day one

**Primary positioning:**
> **Emotion Cards** is a narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions shape both your strategy and your story.

## Target Audience

### Primary audience
- **Deckbuilder / roguelite players** who want strong mechanics and replayability
- **Narrative indie players** looking for more story and emotional payoff than typical card battlers

### Secondary audience
- **Cozy-adjacent strategy players** who like reflective tone, lower aggression, and strong visual identity
- **Creators and press** covering indie strategy, roguelites, deckbuilders, and narrative games

### Audience shorthand
- Players who enjoy **Slay the Spire-style depth** but want **more story, warmth, and emotional identity**
- Players who like **cozy presentation** but still want meaningful decisions and replayable runs

## Key Message Pillars

1. **Emotions become strategy**
   - Cards represent feelings, memories, and reactions
   - Emotional choices are not just story flavor; they shape the run

2. **Narrative deckbuilding with consequence**
   - Runs carry emotional and story weight
   - Different choices and emotional paths lead to different outcomes

3. **Accessible, distinctive tone**
   - Reflective, character-driven, and visually intimate
   - More inviting than grim genre peers without losing strategic depth

4. **Replayable indie premium experience**
   - Run-based structure supports experimentation, mastery, and discovery
   - Clear fit for Steam deckbuilder and narrative indie audiences

## Launch Risks and Mitigations

### 1. "Another deckbuilder" fatigue
**Risk:** Players dismiss the game as genre noise.  
**Mitigation:** Lead every major asset with the emotional-strategy hook and narrative consequence, not generic deckbuilder language.

### 2. Messaging drift across channels
**Risk:** Steam, social, and outreach tell slightly different stories.  
**Mitigation:** Lock one core pitch and reuse it everywhere: *narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions shape strategy and story*.

### 3. Low launch-week coverage density
**Risk:** Press/creator response is lighter than expected.  
**Mitigation:** Prioritize genre-fit creators early, keep backup outreach ready for launch week, and amplify every real quote/clip quickly.

### 4. Wishlist interest does not convert cleanly
**Risk:** Awareness exists, but launch CTA is too soft.  
**Mitigation:** Shift decisively from pre-launch "wishlist now" messaging to launch "out now on Steam" conversion messaging.

### 5. Tone confusion
**Risk:** Cozy-adjacent visuals make the game seem too soft for strategy fans, or too intense for cozy players.  
**Mitigation:** Pair every tone-led asset with clear gameplay readability and every mechanic-led asset with emotional/narrative framing.
