## Purpose

This plan covers how NorthernStar Studio should handle press outreach for **Emotion Cards** from the first public announcement through post-launch support. The goal is not to blast every outlet at once. The goal is to:

- secure the right early coverage
- give reviewers and creators enough time to evaluate the game properly
- build momentum in waves instead of spending all attention on a single day
- keep messaging consistent across press, store page, and community channels

**Positioning to reinforce in every pitch:**
- Emotion Cards is a **roguelike deckbuilder with emotional strategy and narrative weight**
- The strongest angle is **"deckbuilding with meaningful emotional storytelling"**
- The best comparison frame is **"for players who love Slay the Spire-style runs but want stronger narrative identity"**

---

## Core Outreach Principles

1. **Pitch beats, not just the game.** Journalists respond better to a clear story angle than a generic "please cover our game" ask.
2. **Segment by outlet type.** Major news outlets, indie-focused sites, reviewers, and creators all need different timing and assets.
3. **Start with warmest-fit coverage.** A strong article from a relevant indie or deckbuilder-focused outlet matters more than a weak send to a giant site.
4. **Keep outreach human.** Every priority email should reference the writer's beat or recent coverage.
5. **Respect review time.** Review code too late and the plan collapses.
6. **Do not over-follow up.** One strong follow-up is useful. Repeated nudges burn goodwill.

## Audience and Coverage Targets

### Primary audience
- PC players who already play deckbuilders, roguelikes, and strategy indies
- Players looking for a stronger narrative or emotional hook than most card battlers offer
- Indie game fans who respond to distinctive art direction and theme-driven mechanics

### Coverage goals by outlet type
- **Major games press:** announcement visibility, launch news, review consideration
- **Indie-focused press:** feature coverage, previews, interviews, demo impressions
- **Genre specialists:** deckbuilder/roguelike analysis and recommendation traffic
- **Creators/streamers:** hands-on reactions, demo footage, launch-day gameplay, evergreen discovery
- **Industry-facing publications:** studio story, positioning, milestone credibility if there is a notable business angle

## Outreach Phases

## 1) Announce Phase
**Recommended timing:** 8-12 weeks before public demo or major festival beat  
**Primary goal:** introduce the game clearly and establish the hook  
**Main deliverables:** announcement trailer, screenshots, fact sheet, press release, press kit landing page

### What to pitch
- Official reveal of Emotion Cards
- Core premise: emotional deckbuilding + narrative consequence
- Key feature framing: deckbuilding, replayable runs, emotional card identities, character-driven progression
- Wishlist CTA if Steam page is live

### Priority targets
- Broad games news desks that regularly post announcements
- Indie-focused sites likely to cover a reveal from a smaller studio
- Selected creators who like first-look indie reveals

### Success criteria
- 5-10 pieces of announcement coverage across indie and general game press
- A few quality headlines that repeat the core hook accurately
- Early creator interest and Steam wishlist lift

### Notes
This phase should stay simple. Do **not** overload press with deep systems detail. The announcement pitch should answer three things fast:
- What is it?
- Why is it different?
- Why should players care now?

---

## 2) Demo Phase
**Recommended timing:** 4-8 weeks before launch, or aligned with Steam Next Fest / festival participation  
**Primary goal:** prove the game is playable, sticky, and worth preview coverage  
**Main deliverables:** public demo build, updated trailer, fresh screenshots/GIFs, playable talking points, preview email

### What to pitch
- Demo availability
- Specific playable content in the demo
- Hands-on preview opportunity
- Interview access if requested
- Festival hook if applicable

### Best media angles
- "Hands-on with Emotion Cards"
- "A deckbuilder that turns feelings into strategy"
- "Why this indie card battler stands out"

### Success criteria
- Preview coverage from indie outlets and genre-friendly writers
- Creator footage from demo players
- Quotes that can later be used on store page, trailer, and launch materials

### Notes
The demo phase is where the game stops being just a concept. Prioritize outlets and creators who actually play demos and write impressions instead of reposting press releases.

---

## 3) Launch Phase
**Recommended timing:** outreach begins 3-4 weeks before release; embargo lifts on or just before launch  
**Primary goal:** secure reviews, launch articles, and day-one creator coverage  
**Main deliverables:** final review build, review guide, embargo note, launch trailer, release-day press release, updated press kit

### What to pitch
- Review code or final preview code
- Final release date and launch price
- Review embargo details
- Launch trailer and final key art
- "Why now" update: what has improved since demo/announcement

### Success criteria
- Review or recommendation coverage ready near launch
- Reliable day-one articles from outlets already familiar with the game
- Creator coverage that gives the game live discovery during release week

### Notes
This is the most timing-sensitive phase. Review outreach should be highly selective and well organized. If there are not enough keys or support bandwidth, go narrower and better rather than wider and sloppy.

---

## 4) Post-Launch Phase
**Recommended timing:** first 6-8 weeks after launch  
**Primary goal:** extend the life of coverage and create additional reasons to talk about the game  
**Main deliverables:** update notes, accolades graphic, post-launch content beat, player milestone messaging

### What to pitch
- Strong player reception or review score milestone
- Major patch, quality-of-life update, or content update
- New platform announcement if relevant
- Developer commentary / retrospective angle
- "You may have missed it" outreach to slower-moving outlets

### Success criteria
- Secondary wave of feature coverage
- Additional creator coverage from launch holdouts
- Conversion of positive reception into evergreen recommendation coverage

### Notes
Do not disappear after launch week. Good indie games often earn their best coverage after critics, streamers, and communities have had time to recommend them organically.

## Tier 3: Genre and adjacent beats to pursue selectively
These matter if we can tailor the angle.

### Strategy / card battler / roguelike creators
- **Best beats:** hands-on gameplay, build experimentation, replayability, challenge runs
- **Why they matter:** creators often convert better than traditional press for deckbuilders
- **Best phase:** demo, launch, post-launch

### Cozy / narrative indie beats
- **Best beats:** emotional themes, narrative design, accessibility, character-driven framing
- **Why they matter:** if Emotion Cards has softer visual identity or emotional storytelling appeal, this widens audience beyond hardcore deckbuilder players
- **Best phase:** announce, demo, post-launch features

### Industry/business press
- **Best beats:** studio origin story, unusual design direction, milestone performance
- **Why they matter:** not core launch coverage, but useful when there is a real business or trend angle
- **Best phase:** post-launch or milestone-based only

- 6-10 high-resolution screenshots
- 3-5 short gameplay GIFs or short clips
- Announcement trailer link and downloadable file
- Demo trailer or updated gameplay trailer

- Developer/team bio

- Current development status

- Platform list

- "What makes Emotion Cards different" section
- Comparison positioning section for press reference
- Quote sheet once early coverage exists

## Review Key Timing

This is the biggest operational risk in the plan. Reviews are only useful if press has enough time.

## Recommended schedule

### 4 weeks before launch
- Finalize reviewer target list
- Confirm the game build is stable enough for press
- Prepare review guide and embargo language
- Soft-confirm interest from priority reviewers and creators

### 3 weeks before launch
- Send review code to top-priority reviewers and creators
- Include embargo date/time, expected playtime, and support contact
- Track who has accepted, redeemed, and replied

### 2 weeks before launch
- Send second wave to additional outlets and creators
- Follow up with first-wave reviewers only if they previously replied or requested materials
- Refresh FAQ and known issues notes if needed

### 1 week before launch
- Final reminder to confirmed reviewers with any updated build notes
- Do **not** begin panic-blasting extra keys unless the user acquisition plan truly depends on it
- Focus on support, troubleshooting, and creator scheduling

### Launch day
- Embargo lifts
- Send launch-day press release and final asset bundle
- Prioritize responsiveness over new outbound volume

## Review timing rules
- **Minimum acceptable review lead time:** 10-14 days
- **Preferred review lead time:** 2-3 weeks
- **Embargo recommendation:** launch day at release time, or 24 hours before if confidence and review volume support it
- **Do not send same-day review code** unless explicitly positioned as a post-launch key, not a review request

## Recommended Cadence and Follow-Up Rules

## Cadence by phase

### Announce phase cadence
- Initial personalized outreach to top-tier and best-fit indie press
- Same-day or next-day broader send to secondary outlets
- One follow-up after 4-5 business days if no reply

### Demo phase cadence
- Initial send when demo goes live or embargo lifts
- Creator outreach in a staggered wave over 3-5 days rather than one blast
- One follow-up after 3-5 business days for demo-focused outlets, because demo windows move fast

### Launch phase cadence
- Review key outreach 2-3 weeks ahead
- Reminder/update email 5-7 days before embargo lift only to confirmed or previously engaged contacts
- Launch-day release note to full relevant press list
- One post-launch follow-up 5-7 business days later for "in case you missed it" coverage

### Post-launch cadence
- Only outreach when there is a reason: strong reception, update, milestone, event, or platform news
- Space post-launch beats at least 2-3 weeks apart unless there is major news

## Follow-up rules
- Maximum **one follow-up** for cold outreach in most cases
- Maximum **two touches total** unless the journalist replies or asks to stay updated
- If someone says no, archive respectfully and do not keep pushing
- If someone covers the game, thank them and note preferences for future outreach
- If someone opens but does not respond repeatedly, downgrade priority rather than forcing more follow-ups

## Personalization rules
Every priority email should include at least one of:
- a recent article of theirs
- a beat they regularly cover
- a specific reason Emotion Cards fits their audience

Avoid fake familiarity. One honest sentence is enough.

## Inbox discipline rules
- Keep subject lines clear, not cute
- One primary CTA per email
- Put trailer, press kit, and key links high in the message
- Keep the first email under roughly 200 words for most outlets
- Track replies and outcomes in the press contacts document or CRM sheet immediately

## Operational Checklist by Phase

## Before announcement outreach
- Steam page live or nearly live
- Announcement trailer exported and uploaded
- Press kit page complete
- Fact sheet proofread
- Target list segmented by outlet type
- Personalized notes ready for top-priority contacts

## Before demo outreach
- Demo stable and tested
- Clear list of demo content and limitations
- New screenshots/GIFs captured from latest build
- Festival or event timing confirmed
- Creator key/access process tested

## Before launch outreach
- Review build stable
- Review key inventory confirmed
- Embargo date and support owner set
- FAQ and known issues notes drafted
- Launch trailer and release-day materials finalized

## Before post-launch outreach
- Positive quotes or metrics collected
- Update notes packaged into a clear story
- Accolades or reception visual ready if justified
- Next beat selected instead of sending vague check-ins

---

## Recommended Priority Order

If bandwidth is limited, outreach should happen in this order:

1. **Indie and genre-fit press most likely to care**
2. **Deckbuilder / roguelike creators with authentic overlap**
3. **Broad PC outlets**
4. **Major general games outlets**
5. **Long-tail secondary outlets**

That order is deliberate. Emotion Cards is more likely to gain traction from genuine fit than from pure size.

---

## Final Recommendation

For Phase 3, NorthernStar should treat press outreach as a **sequenced campaign** rather than a single launch event.

The strongest plan is:
- **announce with a clean, memorable hook**
- **use the demo to earn hands-on credibility**
- **give reviewers enough time to do real coverage**
- **keep a post-launch story ready so momentum does not die after release week**

If execution is disciplined, Emotion Cards can position itself as **the deckbuilder with emotional identity**, which is a much stronger story than simply being another indie card battler.
