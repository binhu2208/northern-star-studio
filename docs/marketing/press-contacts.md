## Best Practices for Indie Game PR

### Timing Your Outreach

1. **4-6 weeks before announcement**: Tease to build anticipation
2. **2-3 weeks before reveal**: Press release to Tier 1-2 outlets
3. **1 week before launch**: Review code/keys sent, interviews scheduled
4. **Launch week**: Maximum press coverage, social media push
5. **Post-launch**: Follow-up reviews, patch announcements

### Dos and Don'ts

#### DO:
- ✅ Research each journalist's past coverage before contacting
- ✅ Personalize each email - no mass templates
- ✅ Be specific about why you think they'd like your game
- ✅ Offer exclusivity for Tier 1 outlets
- ✅ Include clear call-to-action
- ✅ Follow up politely after 5-7 business days
- ✅ Provide review keys well in advance (2-4 weeks)
- ✅ Create a professional press kit
- ✅ Be responsive to interview requests
- ✅ Build relationships over time

#### DON'T:
- ❌ Send mass emails without personalization
- ❌ Spam every outlet at once
- ❌ Be pushy or demanding coverage
- ❌ Send keys at the last minute
- ❌ Follow up multiple times in one week
- ❌ Pitch without knowing their beat
- ❌ Overhype or make unrealistic claims
- ❌ Forget to thank those who cover you

### Building Your Press Kit

Include in your press kit:
1. **Game logo** (PNG, SVG, various sizes)
2. **Screenshots** (4K, annotated)
3. **Trailer** (YouTube embed + download link)
4. **Fact sheet** (one-page summary)
5. **Developer bio/photos**
6. **Review key instructions**
7. **Contact information**
8. **Social media links**
9. **Steam page link**
10. **Release date/timing**

### Outreach Strategy by Phase

#### Phase 1: Awareness Building (6+ months out)
- Focus: Community building
- Social media presence
- Devlogs on YouTube/Reddit
- Discord server

#### Phase 2: Pre-Announcement (3-6 months out)
- Focus: Influencer seeding
- Send early builds to friendly content creators
- Build grassroots excitement

#### Phase 3: Announcement (1-3 months out)
- Focus: Press outreach
- Press release
- Direct emails to journalists
- Press kit live

#### Phase 4: Launch Prep (1 month out)
- Focus: Review coordination
- Send review keys
- Schedule interviews
- Preview articles

#### Phase 5: Launch Week
- Focus: Maximum visibility
- Day-one reviews
- Streamer coverage
- Social media push
