# Emotion Cards Phase 4 Launch Campaign Plan

**Game:** Emotion Cards  
**Studio:** NorthernStar Studio  
**Prepared by:** Gabe 📊  
**Date:** 2026-03-18  
**Status:** Phase 4 launch planning document

---

## Purpose

This plan turns Phase 4 from "we are launching soon" into a coordinated campaign with clear beats, channel roles, content cadence, and measurable outcomes.

The campaign should do three things well:
- convert existing interest into launch-week sales and reviews
- make **Emotion Cards** easy to understand in one sentence across every channel
- extend momentum beyond day one so the game does not disappear after the first spike

**Core positioning to repeat everywhere:**
- **Emotion Cards** is a **narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions become strategy**
- it is for players who like deckbuilding depth but want stronger story and emotional identity
- the launch CTA is simple: **wishlist / buy on Steam, join Discord, and share your first run**

---

## Launch Campaign Goals

### Primary goals
1. **Convert wishlists into launch-week purchases**
2. **Generate visible social proof fast** through reviews, creator coverage, and community reactions
3. **Give every audience segment a clear reason to care**: strategy players, narrative indie players, cozy-adjacent players, and press/creators
4. **Create a second and third wave of attention** after launch day through updates, clips, quotes, and player stories

### Supporting goals
- grow the Discord from passive followers into an active player hub
- create reusable content assets for launch week and the first month after launch
- collect messaging data on which hooks convert best: emotional strategy, narrative deckbuilder, or replayable runs

### Success definition
A successful launch campaign is not just a good first-day traffic spike. It means:
- Steam page traffic converts efficiently
- launch week creates enough volume for recommendation momentum
- community spaces remain active after launch
- press and creators have enough assets and lead time to cover the game properly

## Campaign Phases and Beats

## 1) Pre-Launch Beats
**Window:** 4 weeks before launch through the day before release  
**Goal:** build urgency, prep reviewers/creators, and make the Steam release feel like an event rather than a surprise

### Beat 1: Release date lock and campaign kickoff (T-4 weeks)
**Focus:** announce the date clearly and start the countdown

**Actions**
- publish a release date announcement across Steam, Discord, X/Twitter, TikTok, YouTube, and press list
- pin/update Steam store capsule messaging and short description if needed
- send press/reviewer outreach with launch date, pitch, key art, trailer, and press kit
- update all social bios to point to the Steam page and Discord

**Core message**
- Emotion Cards launches on **[launch date]**
- wishlist now / follow for launch updates
- emotional deckbuilding + narrative consequence is the differentiator

### Beat 2: Feature spotlight week (T-3 weeks)
**Focus:** explain why the game is different

**Actions**
- run a short feature series: emotional card types, character arcs, run structure, and replayability
- publish 15-30 second gameplay clips for social
- release a one-minute feature overview cut for YouTube and Steam
- continue creator outreach with tailored angles by genre fit

**Core message**
- this is not just another card battler; the emotional theme changes how runs feel and how decisions land

### Beat 3: Community proof + review prep (T-2 weeks)
**Focus:** reduce buyer uncertainty with external validation

**Actions**
- share preview quotes, playtester reactions, or demo takeaways if available
- send second-wave review keys / creator keys
- post FAQ content on Discord and Steam discussions
- prepare launch-day asset pack: short clips, quote cards, review asks, Discord prompts

**Core message**
- players already understand the hook and are responding to the emotional strategy angle

### Beat 4: Countdown week (T-7 days to T-1 day)
**Focus:** urgency and clarity

**Actions**
- daily countdown posts with one specific reason to care each day
- publish launch trailer 2-4 days before release or on release eve, depending on trailer readiness
- final press reminder to engaged contacts only
- confirm creator posting schedule and embargo timing
- post a "how to support at launch" checklist in Discord and on social

**Core message**
- launching in days
- wishlist now if not ready to buy on day one
- join Discord to share your first run and get launch updates

---

## 2) Launch Week Beats
**Window:** launch day through day 7  
**Goal:** concentrate attention, make the game look active/alive, and sustain conversation long enough for algorithmic and social discovery

### Launch Day
**Focus:** coordinated visibility burst

**Actions**
- publish Steam launch announcement and ensure store page is fully current
- post the launch trailer across all owned channels
- activate Discord community prompts: first impressions, favorite cards, screenshots, bugs/support channel
- push short-form clips throughout the day instead of a single post-and-disappear burst
- send launch press release and "now available" note to relevant contacts
- amplify creator coverage and media mentions in real time

**Core message**
- Emotion Cards is out now on Steam
- buy now / launch discount if offered
- leave a review after your first session

### Day 2-3
**Focus:** social proof and community activation

**Actions**
- highlight early player quotes, clips, and screenshots
- post "what kind of run are you building?" prompts and card-choice polls
- share a short strategy tip thread/video for new players
- respond quickly to questions and bugs to show the game is supported

**Core message**
- people are playing, talking, and discovering different emotional builds

### Day 4-5
**Focus:** broaden discovery beyond launch-day audience

**Actions**
- pitch/repitch "in case you missed it" launch coverage to slower-moving outlets
- publish a creator/press roundup on social and Discord
- release a deeper YouTube video: launch recap, dev commentary, or beginner guide
- clip the best reactions for TikTok/X reuse

**Core message**
- here is why people are paying attention, and here is what makes the game stick

### Weekend / Day 6-7
**Focus:** extend retention and recommendation energy

**Actions**
- run community events: screenshot thread, favorite-card vote, or challenge run prompt
- encourage Steam reviews explicitly and politely
- share a "week one roadmap" update if there are early fixes or quality-of-life changes coming

**Core message**
- the game is live, supported, and worth jumping into now while the conversation is active

---

## 3) Post-Launch Beats
**Window:** weeks 2-6 after release  
**Goal:** keep the game discoverable, give holdout audiences a new reason to look, and convert early player response into durable credibility

### Beat 1: Reception and support update (Week 2)
**Actions**
- share launch-week metrics internally and approved external milestones publicly
- publish an update post covering fixes, quality-of-life improvements, and community thanks
- create an accolades/reception visual if quotes justify it

### Beat 2: Creator/community momentum (Week 3-4)
**Actions**
- resurface standout streams, strategy clips, and fan reactions
- publish a dev commentary or behind-the-scenes video about emotional card design
- continue targeted outreach to creators who prefer covering games after launch

### Beat 3: Discovery refresh (Week 4-6)
**Actions**
- ship a meaningful update, challenge mode, balance patch, or community-facing improvement if available
- tie outreach to a real news hook rather than generic "please cover us"
- revisit Steam events/announcements if there is a discount, update, or milestone worth promoting

**Post-launch rule:** do not go quiet after launch week. The campaign should transition from hype to proof.

## Channel Plan

## Steam
**Role:** highest-conversion channel and source of truth for purchase intent

**Objectives**
- maximize wishlist-to-purchase conversion
- support discovery through reviews, activity, and announcements
- make the store page answer "why this game" in seconds

**Key actions**
- release date announcement
- launch announcement on day 1
- update store assets and trailers before launch
- post news/update items during week 1 and post-launch
- prompt for reviews after players have had time to play

**Best content types**
- trailer
- screenshots/GIFs
- review quotes
- patch/update notes
- community highlights if suitable for announcements

## Discord
**Role:** community retention, support, and social proof engine

**Objectives**
- turn followers into active advocates
- gather first impressions, FAQs, and bug reports quickly
- create a place where new buyers see real player activity

**Key actions**
- launch countdown posts and @everyone only for major beats
- dedicated channels for announcements, first-runs, feedback, bugs, and strategy
- launch day welcome message + review request + support links
- lightweight community events during week 1

**Best content types**
- behind-the-scenes notes
- polls
- player screenshots
- favorite-card prompts
- patch notes and support updates

## X / Twitter
**Role:** fast-moving public awareness, networking, press/creator amplification

**Objectives**
- keep the game visible during countdown and launch week
- amplify creators and coverage quickly
- test which hooks get the best engagement and click-through

**Key actions**
- daily launch-week posting cadence
- short gameplay clips, quote cards, countdown assets, and launch trailer
- reply-posting around creator coverage, genre conversations, and launch mentions
- pin the strongest CTA post during the campaign window

**Best content types**
- 10-30 second clips
- concise hook-led posts
- review quote cards
- launch day thread
- creator and press reposts

## TikTok
**Role:** top-of-funnel awareness and hook testing through short-form video

**Objectives**
- surface the game to players who did not see the Steam page or X posts
- identify clips that naturally earn watch time and shares
- turn emotional-card mechanics into instantly understandable moments

**Key actions**
- post 3-5 short clips per week pre-launch, then 1-2 per day in launch week if asset volume supports it
- prioritize strongest visual/emotional payoff moments in first 2 seconds
- reuse creator reactions and surprising card interactions where permissions allow

**Best content types**
- card combo reveals
- before/after emotional decision moments
- rapid run highlights
- dev voiceover explaining one mechanic cleanly

## YouTube
**Role:** durable discovery, trailers, and deeper explanation

**Objectives**
- host high-quality launch and feature videos
- provide evergreen content for press, creators, and buyers doing extra research
- support SEO over the month around launch

**Key actions**
- release date video / trailer
- feature overview video
- launch trailer
- optional beginner guide or dev commentary video in launch week / week 2

**Best content types**
- 60-90 second trailers
- 3-6 minute feature explainers
- developer commentary
- community reaction montage or strategy starter guide

## Press
**Role:** credibility, reach, and external validation

**Objectives**
- secure launch coverage, previews/reviews, and post-launch follow-up coverage
- provide quotes and references that improve conversion on other channels
- build relationships with genre-fit outlets and creators

**Key actions**
- pre-launch release date pitch
- review/code outreach 2-3 weeks ahead where possible
- launch day press release
- post-launch "in case you missed it" and update-led outreach

**Best content types**
- press kit
- fact sheet
- review guide
- launch trailer
- concise personalized pitches

## Content Cadence

## Recommended weekly cadence: pre-launch (final 4 weeks)
- **Steam:** 1 major announcement/update as needed
- **Discord:** 3-5 posts per week, heavier in final countdown week
- **X/Twitter:** 5-7 posts per week minimum, plus replies/amplification
- **TikTok:** 3-5 posts per week
- **YouTube:** 1 video every 1-2 weeks
- **Press:** 1-2 organized outreach waves, not daily drip spam

## Recommended cadence: launch week
- **Steam:** launch day post + 1 follow-up/news item if needed
- **Discord:** daily activity, multiple touchpoints on launch day
- **X/Twitter:** 2-4 posts per day across launch day and early week
- **TikTok:** 1-2 posts per day if enough strong clips exist
- **YouTube:** launch trailer + 1 support/explainer/community video
- **Press:** launch-day send + one targeted follow-up later in the week

## Recommended cadence: post-launch (weeks 2-6)
- **Steam:** update posts tied to real patches/news
- **Discord:** near-daily community/support presence
- **X/Twitter:** 4-6 posts per week
- **TikTok:** 3-4 posts per week
- **YouTube:** 2-4 videos per month
- **Press:** only when there is a genuine new angle

## Content mix
A healthy mix for the campaign:
- **35% gameplay hook content** — what the game is and why it is interesting
- **25% social proof** — quotes, reactions, creators, player screenshots
- **20% education** — how systems work, beginner-friendly explanations, feature spotlights
- **10% community** — polls, prompts, fan responses
- **10% urgency / CTA** — release date, countdown, launch, review asks, discounts

## KPI Suggestions

These should be tracked weekly during pre-launch and daily during launch week where possible.

## Steam KPIs
- wishlist growth rate by week
- wishlist-to-purchase conversion during launch window
- store page visits and external click sources
- launch-week unit sales
- review count and recent review sentiment
- trailer views / completion if available

## Community KPIs
- Discord member growth
- launch-week active members / posts per day
- number of player screenshots, feedback posts, and first-run shares
- support response time and unresolved issue count

## Social KPIs
- impressions and engagement rate by channel
- click-through rate to Steam page
- video watch time / completion rate for TikTok and YouTube
- saves/shares on short-form posts
- follower growth during countdown and launch week

## Press / creator KPIs
- number of articles, previews, reviews, or creator videos/streams
- coverage quality: accurate positioning, links included, sentiment
- estimated reach from creator/press mentions
- number of reusable quotes/clips generated

## Suggested campaign targets
Use these as planning benchmarks, then refine when real baseline data exists:
- steady pre-launch wishlist growth week over week
- visible day-one review volume rather than waiting a week for social proof
- at least one meaningful content beat per channel during launch week
- enough creator/press coverage to produce daily amplification opportunities for the first 5-7 days

## Final Recommendation

Phase 4 should be run like a **four-week countdown plus a four-week proof cycle**, not a one-day blast.

The strongest version of this campaign is:
- **clear release-date urgency before launch**
- **tight coordination across Steam, Discord, X, TikTok, YouTube, and press**
- **heavy launch-week social proof and responsiveness**
- **post-launch beats that turn reception into ongoing discovery**

If execution stays disciplined, Emotion Cards can launch with a positioning that is easy to remember and easy to repeat:

**A narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions become strategy.**
