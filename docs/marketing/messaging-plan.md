# Emotion Cards Messaging Plan

**Game:** Emotion Cards  
**Studio:** NorthernStar Studio  
**Prepared by:** Gabe 📊  
**Branch Target:** `release/v0.5.0`  
**Status:** Draft

---

## Purpose

This document defines the core launch messaging for **Emotion Cards** so Steam, Discord, social, and press all sound like they are talking about the same game.

The job of this plan is simple:
- make the game easy to understand in one sentence
- make the game feel distinct from generic deckbuilders
- give every channel a version of the message that fits how people actually read there
- keep CTA language consistent across wishlist and launch beats

This should be used alongside the broader campaign, store, and outreach docs. Those explain *when* to post and *what assets* to use. This document explains *what we keep saying*.

---

## Core Positioning Statement

**Emotion Cards is a narrative roguelike deckbuilder where your feelings shape both your strategy and your story.**

### Short version
**A narrative deckbuilder where emotions become strategy.**

### What this positioning needs to do
This line works because it carries the three things we most need players to understand fast:
- **genre clarity** — it is a roguelike deckbuilder
- **mechanical differentiation** — emotions are not just theme; they affect strategy
- **narrative hook** — story outcomes matter, not just combat efficiency

### Positioning guardrails
We should avoid messaging that frames the game as:
- only a cozy story game
- only a hardcore card battler
- a vague “emotional experience” without clear gameplay

The best version of the pitch always keeps **strategy + story** in the same sentence.

## Message Pillars

## 1) Emotions are gameplay, not just theme
**Core idea:** the emotional concept is mechanically meaningful.

**Why it matters:** players need to know this is not a thin reskin. The emotional layer is the design hook.

**Proof points**
- cards, choices, and encounters are built around emotional states
- feelings shape how runs develop and how decks evolve
- the emotional framing changes how decisions land, not just how they look

**Useful language**
- Your feelings shape your deck
- Emotions are part of the strategy
- Every run asks not just what is optimal, but what emotional path you commit to

## 2) Strong narrative identity inside a replayable deckbuilder
**Core idea:** Emotion Cards offers the replayability deckbuilder fans want, with stronger story and character weight than the genre usually delivers.

**Why it matters:** this is one of the cleanest ways to differentiate from better-known deckbuilders.

**Proof points**
- narrative consequence sits alongside run-based replayability
- choices carry emotional and story weight
- players who want both systems and feeling have a clear reason to care

**Useful language**
- Replayable runs with narrative consequence
- A deckbuilder with story weight
- For players who want strategy depth without losing emotional stakes

## 3) A personal, reflective tone without losing tension
**Core idea:** the game feels intimate and human, but it still needs to read as a real strategy game with stakes.

**Why it matters:** this widens appeal beyond pure strategy players without misleading players into expecting a passive visual novel.

**Proof points**
- character-driven framing
- reflective, emotionally grounded tone
- meaningful conflict, not just comfort vibes

**Useful language**
- Reflective, character-driven deckbuilding
- Cozy in tone, but not consequence-free
- Intimate story, real decisions

## 4) Easy to pitch to two adjacent audiences
**Core idea:** the game can speak to deckbuilder fans and narrative indie players at the same time.

**Why it matters:** launch messaging should expand the audience without diluting the premise.

**Audience A: deckbuilder / roguelike players**
- hook them with strategy, runs, builds, and replayability

**Audience B: narrative / emotional indie players**
- hook them with character stakes, emotional choices, and story outcomes

**Useful language**
- For players who love deckbuilding depth and stronger narrative identity
- A run-based strategy game with emotional consequence
- A story-forward deckbuilder, not a story-only game

## Channel-Specific Messaging Notes

## Steam
**Role:** conversion-first. Be clear fast.

### What Steam copy should emphasize
- genre clarity in the first line
- emotional strategy as the unique mechanic/identity
- replayability and narrative consequence together
- reasons to wishlist or buy now

### Steam messaging priorities
1. What kind of game is this?
2. What makes it different from other deckbuilders?
3. Why is it worth wishlisting / buying today?

### Steam copy tone
- direct
- readable in seconds
- benefit-led, not lore-heavy
- no fluffy “an unforgettable journey” phrasing unless backed by clear gameplay

### Steam-ready sample lines
- **Emotion Cards is a narrative roguelike deckbuilder where your feelings shape both your strategy and your story.**
- **Build emotion-driven decks, make difficult choices, and discover new paths across replayable runs.**
- **Wishlist now to follow launch updates and be ready when Emotion Cards arrives on Steam.**
- **Out now on Steam — build your deck, shape your story, and see how each emotional path changes the run.**

### Steam watch-outs
Avoid copy that sounds:
- too abstract
- too literary
- too focused on narrative at the expense of gameplay clarity

If a Steam visitor cannot identify “deckbuilder” within seconds, the messaging is failing.

## Discord
**Role:** community energy, retention, and advocacy.

### What Discord messaging should emphasize
- direct conversation with players
- first-run excitement
- belonging and participation
- sharing builds, choices, screenshots, and reactions

### Discord tone
- warmer and more human than Steam
- less polished, more conversational
- invite action and response

### Discord sample lines
- **Emotion Cards is live. Jump in, share your first run, and tell us which emotional path you leaned into.**
- **If you’ve been waiting on a deckbuilder with more story weight, this is the one.**
- **Show us your favorite card combo, hardest choice, or the moment the run really clicked for you.**
- **Wishlist on Steam, then hang out here for updates, strategy talk, and launch-week events.**

### Discord message prompts
- What emotion carried your run?
- Which choice hurt the most?
- What card combo are you building around?
- What surprised you on your first run?

### Discord watch-outs
Don’t sound overproduced. Discord should feel like an active studio/community space, not a press release pasted into chat.

## X / Twitter
**Role:** fast hook + repeat exposure + amplification.

### What X messaging should emphasize
- one strong line per post
- visually supported hooks
- easy-to-repost framing
- launch urgency or curiosity gap

### X tone
- concise
- punchy
- confident
- built around one idea, not five

### X sample post directions
- **A narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions become strategy. Emotion Cards is coming to Steam. Wishlist now.**
- **What if your deckbuilder cared how you feel? Emotion Cards turns emotional choices into real strategy.**
- **Build your deck. Shape your story. Emotion Cards is out now on Steam.**
- **Replayable runs, emotional stakes, difficult choices. Emotion Cards launches on Steam [date].**

### X formatting advice
- keep the first sentence strong enough to stand alone
- pair with gameplay clip, screenshot, or trailer cutdown
- use CTA early, not buried at the end
- avoid long threaded explanation unless it is a feature spotlight

### X watch-outs
If the post needs a paragraph to explain the hook, it is too soft for the platform.

## Press
**Role:** credibility and accurate framing.

### What press messaging should emphasize
- genre + differentiation immediately
- why this matters now
- what coverage angle makes sense
- who the likely audience is

### Best press framing angles
- **A deckbuilder with emotional strategy and narrative consequence**
- **A story-forward roguelike deckbuilder for players who want more narrative identity in the genre**
- **An indie strategy game that bridges deckbuilder depth and emotional storytelling**

### Press boilerplate-style line
- **Emotion Cards is a narrative roguelike deckbuilder from NorthernStar Studio where emotions shape both combat decisions and story outcomes.**

### Press pitch support language
- for players who enjoy replayable deckbuilding runs but want stronger narrative weight
- emotional choices are reflected in both strategy and tone
- designed to stand out from generic card battlers through a more personal, story-driven identity

### Press watch-outs
Avoid overselling with vague prestige language. Press needs a clean angle they can headline, not a cloud of adjectives.

## Message Consistency Rules

To keep the launch coherent, repeat these ideas constantly:
- **narrative roguelike deckbuilder**
- **emotions shape strategy**
- **story and gameplay are linked**
- **wishlist now / out now on Steam**

### Phrases worth reusing often
- emotions become strategy
- your feelings shape the run
- build your deck, shape your story
- replayable runs with narrative consequence
- a deckbuilder with emotional stakes

### Phrases to use carefully or avoid
- “cozy” on its own without strategy context
- “emotional journey” without gameplay context
- “immersive experience” because it says almost nothing
- “genre-defying” because it usually weakens clarity

## Final Recommendation

Emotion Cards should not try to win on vague emotional branding alone. That would make it sound softer and less distinctive than it is.

The strongest launch messaging is the version that keeps saying, clearly and repeatedly:
- this is a **deckbuilder**
- emotions are part of the **strategy**
- the story gives those decisions **weight**

That combination is the point.

**Best core line:**  
**A narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions become strategy.**
