# Game Proposal - Emotion Cards Two

## Overview
- **Project:** Emotion Cards Two
- **Genre:** Narrative deckbuilder / roguelike strategy card game
- **Target Platform:** PC first (Steam), with later platform expansion depending on traction and scope
- **Target Audience:** Fans of indie deckbuilders, narrative strategy players, and players drawn to emotionally themed gameplay systems

## Unique Selling Point
Emotion Cards Two expands the original Emotion Cards concept into a more confident sequel: stronger emotional mechanics, broader content variety, and improved narrative integration. Its core differentiator remains the same — emotions are the strategic system, not just the story wrapper.

## Core Gameplay
The core loop for Emotion Cards Two is:
1. Begin a run with a selected character or emotional framework
2. Build a deck around feelings, reactions, and synergistic emotional states
3. Progress through branching encounters, tactical battles, and narrative choices
4. Unlock new cards, emotional combinations, and story content across repeated runs
5. Improve mastery through deck refinement, encounter knowledge, and long-term progression

The design aim is to make the sequel feel broader and smoother without losing the emotional identity that makes the concept memorable.

## Market Research
- **Direct comparables:** Slay the Spire, Griftlands, Wildfrost, Monster Train, Inscryption
- **Adjacent comparables:** Cozy narrative indies, relationship-driven strategy games, replayable progression games with strong thematic hooks
- **Market opportunity:** The deckbuilder category remains active but competitive. Emotion Cards Two can stand out by emphasizing emotional mechanics, character-driven narrative, and stronger sequel polish.
- **Why this can work:** Players continue to reward indie deckbuilders that are easy to pitch, mechanically clean, and visually distinct.

## Business Requirement Analysis
- The game needs a sharper sequel positioning than the original: clearly improved, not just more content
- PC-first scope remains the most practical path for validating market demand and production efficiency
- The project should support a clean Steam-facing message and trailer strategy from day one
- The emotional systems need to remain understandable enough for marketing screenshots, clips, and creator coverage
- The sequel should be scoped so the team can achieve polish and clear differentiation without overextending production

## Market Trends Analysis
- The genre is crowded, but quality sequels still perform when they present a stronger hook and better execution
- Emotional/narrative framing can create differentiation in a market dominated by fantasy and abstract combat themes
- Discoverability depends heavily on first-impression clarity: art direction, UI readability, and pitch line matter a lot
- Sequel positioning can reduce risk if the market understands why the second game is meaningfully better than the first

## User Experience Analysis
- Emotional mechanics must be readable quickly and feel tactically meaningful
- Onboarding should explain the sequel’s new systems without overwhelming returning or new players
- Story beats should enhance pacing, not interrupt the strategic flow
- Visual feedback should make emotional states, card synergy, and consequences obvious at a glance
- The target UX outcome is a game that feels emotionally distinctive, strategically satisfying, and easier to recommend than the first title

## Estimated Timeline
- **Phase 1: Concept refinement + prototype** — validate sequel improvements and emotional mechanics
- **Phase 2: Vertical slice** — lock presentation, sample content, and player-facing differentiators
- **Phase 3: Production + launch prep** — content expansion, balancing, QA, and release asset preparation

## Budget Estimate
- **Lean indie sequel scope:** Low-to-mid six figures depending on art scope, narrative breadth, and content volume
- Main cost drivers:
  - systems and gameplay implementation
  - encounter/card content production
  - art/UI improvements
  - writing and narrative integration
  - QA, balancing, and launch marketing materials

## Recommendation
Proceed as a **focused PC-first sequel** with early emphasis on proving the upgrade over the original game. The strongest commercial path is to validate:
1. clearer emotional strategy depth,
2. better run-to-run variety, and
3. stronger market-facing positioning.

If those three land, Emotion Cards Two has a solid chance to compete as a differentiated indie deckbuilder.
