# Wren Character Design — Grief & Memory Arc

**Character:** Wren  
**Emotion Family:** Shadow  
**Theme:** Learning to carry loss without being consumed by it  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Date:** March 17, 2026  
**Author:** Hideo 🎮

---

## 1. Character Background

### Who is Wren?

Wren is a person in their early thirties who experienced a profound loss — but not through death. The person they loved is still alive, just... no longer *them*. Maybe a relationship ended. Maybe someone changed in ways that made them a stranger. Maybe they grew apart so gradually that Wren didn't notice until the distance was insurmountable.

The grief Wren carries is for someone who's still breathing.

### Why They're Here

Wren arrives at the **Garden of Echoes** — a place where emotions take physical form — with no memory of how they got there. A gentle guide (the same one who greets all visitors) explains: *"You carry something heavy. Here, you can face it."*

Wren's goal: Not to "get over" the loss, but to learn how to carry it — to honor the memory without being trapped by it.

---

## 2. Emotional Starting State

### Dominant Emotion: **Grief** (Shadow Family)

Wren begins with a deck weighted toward Shadow emotions, reflecting their current inability to process the loss:

**Starting Deck (15 cards):**
- 4x "Pretend Everything's Fine" (Denial — ignore the pain)
- 3x "Heavy Heart" (Grief — the weight of absence)
- 3x "Ghost of What Was" (Longing — idealized memory)
- 2x "If Only" (Bargaining — replaying could-have-beens)
- 2x "Haunted" (Melancholy — memories that won't release)
- 1x "Faint Smile" (Shadow/Warmth — moment of warmth, quickly suppressed)

**Emotional Capacity:** 3 plays per turn (burdened but not yet crushed)

### Starting Narrative State

Wren believes:
- They should have done more to save the relationship
- The person they lost would be disappointed in them
- Moving on means forgetting, and forgetting means betraying the memory
- They're alone in this kind of grief — others don't understand

---

## 3. Emotional Journey (6 Phases)

### Phase 1: Denial

*"Nothing changed. Everything's fine."*

Wren starts here — pretending the loss didn't happen, or that it doesn't matter. The first encounters force them to acknowledge that something is wrong, even if they can't name it yet.

**Key Mechanic:** Avoidance — Wren can ignore damage/debuffs for a turn, but the cost accumulates.

**Emotional State:** Numb, dissociated, functioning but not present.

---

### Phase 2: Weight

*"I can't shake this heaviness."*

The numbness cracks. Wren feels the full weight of the absence — not just the person, but the future they imagined, the version of themselves that existed in relation to them.

**Key Mechanic:** Burden — gain benefits from carrying weight, but lose speed/agility.

**Emotional State:** Exhausted, slow, burdened but present.

---

### Phase 3: Haunting

*"They won't leave my mind."*

Memories become intrusive. Wren replays moments, questions decisions, imagines alternate outcomes. The past feels more real than the present.

**Key Mechanic:** Memory tokens — Wren can summon echoes of the past, but they fade and can hurt as much as help.

**Emotional State:** Obsessed, fragmented, lost in time.

---

### Phase 4: Bargaining

*"If I just tried harder, maybe..."*

Wren starts searching for ways to undo the loss — replaying arguments in their head, imagining what they could have done differently. Hope mixes with desperation.

**Key Mechanic:** Conditional power — cards become stronger but only under specific (often impossible) conditions.

**Emotional State:** Desperate, stuck, self-blame emerging.

---

### Phase 5: Shadows

*"The loss is real. I can't outrun it anymore."*

Wren stops fighting the grief and begins to sit with it. The darkness is still there, but it's no longer all-consuming. The first glimpses of acceptance appear — not as victory, but as survival.

**Key Mechanic:** Transformation — negative effects can be converted into positive ones.

**Emotional State:** Grieving but grounded, sad but stable.

---

### Phase 6: Wren

*"I carry them with me. That's enough."*

The final phase. Wren transforms grief into something sustainable — a memory that enriches rather than burdens. Like a wren carrying songs across distances, Wren carries the past forward without being weighed down.

**Key Mechanic:** Legacy — memory tokens become permanent buffs, enhancing future cards.

**Emotional State:** Peaceful sadness, carrying forward, growth without erasure.

## 4. Card Concepts (20 Cards)

### Phase 1: Denial (Cards 1-4)

Cards in this phase represent Wren's attempts to avoid the pain. They're not sustainable — the avoidance works temporarily but accumulates hidden costs.

---

#### Card 1: Pretend

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Defense (Shadow) |
| **Energy Cost** | 1 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Ignore all damage and debuffs this turn. Gain 1 "Defer" (next turn, take +2 damage). |
| **Flavor** *"If I don't look at it, it's not really there."* |

**Design Notes:** Short-term gain, long-term cost. Sets up Wren's core tension — avoidance eventually catches up.

---

#### Card 2: Everything's Fine

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Heal (Shadow) |
| **Energy Cost** | 2 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Heal 5 HP. At the end of next turn, lose 3 HP. |
| **Flavor** *"I smiled. That's what you do."* |

**Design Notes:** Temporary healing that rebounds. The denial phase can't provide real recovery — only postponement.

---

#### Card 3: Photo Album

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Utility (Shadow) |
| **Energy Cost** | 1 |
| **Damage** | 0 |
| **Effect** | Draw 2 cards. Add 1 "Memory Fragment" to your hand (treat as any Shadow card, but fades at end of combat). |
| **Flavor** *"Looking at old pictures, pretending nothing changed."* |

**Design Notes:** Denial draws on memory but doesn't engage with it meaningfully. The fragment is useful but temporary.

---

#### Card 4: Ghost

| Attribute | Value |

**Design Notes:** First summon card. The ghost is helpful but self-destructive — clinging to memories hurts Wren.

## 6. How Wren Complements Maya and Ember

### Maya (Shadow — Resentment & Reconciliation)

**Connection:** Both are Shadow-family characters, but they process loss differently.

| Aspect | Maya | Wren |
|--------|------|------|
| **Loss Type** | Person is alive but relationship fractured | Person is different but still alive |
| **Core Emotion** | Resentment (anger at being wronged) | Grief (sadness at what changed) |
| **Direction** | Blames other person | Blames self |
| **Healing Path** | Understanding the other's perspective | Accepting self without the other |
| **Deck Style** | Confrontation-focused | Memory-focused |

**Synergy:** Wren's memory mechanics pair with Maya's revelation system — understanding the full truth requires facing memory. Wren's Acceptance can help Maya process resentment into acceptance.

**Contrast:** Maya learns to let go of blame; Wren learns to carry without being consumed. Both reach peace through different paths.

---

### Ember (Fire — Anger & Redemption)

**Connection:** Opposite emotional families, but complementary arcs.

| Aspect | Ember | Wren |
|--------|-------|------|
| **Core Emotion** | Anger at external injustice | Grief at internal loss |
| **Direction** | Outward, destructive | Inward, consuming |
| **Healing Path** | Learning controlled use | Learning to transform |
| **Deck Style** | Heat management | Memory management |

**Synergy:** Ember's cooling mechanics help balance Wren's weight. Wren's "If Only" (conditional power) pairs with Ember's "Trembling Spark" (low-heat reward) — both reward constraint.

**Contrast:** Ember transforms anger into creation; Wren transforms grief into memory. Both learn that intense emotions can be tools, not masters.

---

### Team Dynamic

Together, the three characters form a complete emotional journey:

- **Maya** — How to reconcile with others
- **Ember** — How to control inner fire
- **Wren** — How to carry what can't be changed

**Multi-Character Synergy (Future):**
- Wren + Maya: Memory Echoes can reveal truth for Maya's confrontations
- Wren + Ember: Carrying grief doesn't prevent finding new fire
- All three: Balance leads to the complete emotional spectrum

## 7. Design Notes

### Theme Alignment

Wren's arc is about **grief without loss** — the unique pain of mourning someone who's still alive. This is underexplored in games and deeply relatable.

**Key Insight:** The goal isn't to "get over" grief. It's to transform it from weight into legacy. The final phase isn't forgetting — it's carrying forward.

### Card Design Philosophy

1. **Denial → Weight → Haunting → Bargaining → Shadows → Wren** mirrors the Kübler-Ross model but adapts it for ongoing (non-death) loss.

2. **Memory Echoes** are Wren's signature mechanic — they're both burden and power. The player chooses: avoid echoes (safer, weaker) or embrace them (risky, transformative).

3. **Deferred costs** represent how unprocessed grief catches up. Early-phase cards give now and take later. Late-phase cards give sustainably.

4. **Hybrid cards** emerge at the turning point (Acceptance, Flight) and dominate the final phase. This mirrors real growth: Shadow and Warmth aren't opposites — they're connected.

### Balancing Considerations

- Phase 1 (Denial) should feel appealing but unsustainable
- Phase 2 (Weight) should feel punishing but mechanically interesting
- Phase 3 (Haunting) should feel chaotic but powerful
- Phase 4 (Bargaining) should feel desperate but have high ceiling
- Phase 5-6 (Shadows → Wren) should feel like earned transformation

### Future Expansion

- **Tide (Storm)** completes the quartet with trust/vulnerability
- Cross-character encounters where all three can appear
- "Carry Forward" mechanic for meta-progression across characters
