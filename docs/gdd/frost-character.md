# Frost Character Design

**Theme:** Fear & Courage  
**Emotion Family:** Ice  
**Arc:** From frozen by fear to acting despite fear

---

## Character Background

Someone who experienced something terrifying — an accident, a close call, a moment where everything fell apart. The memory froze them. They stopped taking risks, stopped trying, because trying meant possibly failing, and failure meant feeling that terror again.

Now they must learn that courage isn't the absence of fear — it's moving forward even when every instinct screams to stay frozen.

---

## Emotional Journey

### Phase 1: Freeze
The initial traumatic moment. Time stops. The body locks. This is the default state when threatened — freeze, don't move, maybe the danger won't see you.

### Phase 2: Shatter
The frozen state cracks. Fear becomes overwhelming, uncontrolled. Panic. The world is dangerous, anyone could hurt them, better to break before being broken.

### Phase 3: Cold Shoulder
Pushing people away becomes protection. Isolation feels safer than connection. If nothing can touch them, nothing can hurt them. But the cold spreads.

### Phase 4: Frostbite
The cost of freezing becomes clear. Relationships atrophied. Opportunities lost. They survived, but they didn't really live. The cold has done its damage.

### Phase 5: Thaw
First signs of melting. A moment of courage. Reaching out despite fear. Small risks. The ice begins to crack — terrifying, but necessary.

### Phase 6: Frost
Not warm, not cold — balanced. They know fear now, truly understand it. But they've learned to act despite it. They're like frost: delicate, temporary, but present and real. Beauty in cold places.

## Card Concepts (20 Cards)

### Freeze Phase (Cards 1-4)

**1. Frozen Stance**
- *Type:* Defense
- *Effect:* Block all damage this turn, but gain no energy. You're completely still.
- *Design:* The "deer in headlights" response — sometimes freezing is the only option.

**2. Ice Shard**
- *Type:* Attack
- *Effect:* Quick strike, deals damage. If you took damage last turn, +2 damage.
- *Design:* Fear sharpens you. The memory of pain makes you react faster.

**3. Breath Misted**
- *Type:* Utility
- *Effect:* Look at the top 3 cards of opponent's deck. You can rearrange them.
- *Design:* Hypervigilance — always scanning for threats.

**4. Whitened Knuckles**
- *Type:* Buff
- *Effect:* Gain +2 defense. If you take damage, gain +1 extra defense for this combat.
- *Design:* White-knuckling through fear — gripping so tight your hands turn white.

---

### Shatter Phase (Cards 5-10)

**5. Panic**
- *Type:* Debuff
- *Effect:* Gain 2 fear tokens. Each fear token gives -1 to all actions. Remove one fear token each turn.
- *Design:* Overwhelmed state — fear spirals.

**6. Shatter**
- *Type:* Attack
- *Effect:* Deal damage to all enemies. You also take damage equal to half.
- *Design:* Breaking apart — the controlled freeze becomes uncontrolled chaos.

**7. Hyperventilate**
- *Type:* Status
- *Effect:* Skip your next turn's draw. Gain 2 energy this turn to compensate.
- *Design:* The body takes over — breathing goes wrong, mind goes foggy.

**8. Chattering Teeth**
- *Type:* Defense
- *Effect:* Small block, but copy it to an ally. You shake together.
- *Design:* Shared fear is slightly less scary.

**9. Frostbite**
- *Type:* Attack/Debuff
- *Effect:* Deal damage and apply "Frostbite" — takes 1 damage each turn for 3 turns.
- *Design:* The cold doesn't just hurt once — it lingers.

**10. Winter Blindness**
- *Type:* Utility
- *Effect:* Both players shuffle their hands back into deck and draw 2. Reset the board state somewhat.
- *Design:* Sometimes fear makes everything white and unclear — a fresh start.

---

### Cold Shoulder Phase (Cards 11-14)

**11. Walls**
- *Type:* Defense
- *Effect:* Gain block. Cannot receive ally support this turn. Reject help.
- *Design:* Pushing people away "for protection."

**12. Distance**
- *Type:* Utility
- *Effect:* Move away from target enemy. They can't follow this turn.
- *Design:* Creating space is safety.

**13. Frost Form**
- *Type:* Buff
- *Effect:* You can't be targeted by enemies, but also can't attack. Draw extra cards.
- *Design:* Become untouchable — and ineffective.

**14. Alone**
- *Type:* Status
- *Effect:* Gain +3 power when you have no allies. Lose 2 power if you have allies.
- *Design:* The belief that isolation equals safety.

---

### Frostbite Phase (Cards 15-17)

**15. What Was Lost**
- *Type:* Reflection
- *Effect:* Look at your discard pile. Choose a card to shuffle back into deck. The cost? Take 2 damage for each time you've frozen this combat.
- *Design:* Seeing what the freezing cost you.

**16. Icicle**
- *Type:* Attack
- *Effect:* A sharp, single strike. Deals 4 damage but you take 1.
- *Design:* The cost of acting — there's always risk.

**17. Glacier**
- *Type:* Defense
- *Effect:* Massive block. Takes 3 turns to build up but lasts a long time.
- *Design:* Slow to change, but when you do, it's massive.

---

### Thaw Phase (Cards 18-19)

**18. First Step**
- *Type:* Utility
- *Effect:* Gain 1 courage. Courage can stack and negates fear tokens (1 for 1).
- *Design:* The beginning of courage — small but real.

**19. Shivering**
- *Type:* Status
- *Effect:* You're "shaking" — gain block, but take 1 damage. The shaking is fear AND cold.
- *Design:* You're doing something despite being terrified.

---

### Frost Phase (Card 20)

**20. Frost**
- *Type:* Ultimate
- *Effect:* Transform all your fear tokens into courage. Gain block equal to the amount transformed. Your next attack deals bonus damage equal to courage.
- *Design:* The final form — fear understood, fear transmuted. Not warm, not cold. Balanced. Present.

## Synergy with Existing Characters

### Maya (Reconciliation)
- **Frost + Maya:** Both deal with past trauma. Maya reconciles with others; Frost reconciles with themselves. Maya brings warmth that slowly melts Frost's defenses. Excellent defensive synergy — Maya's healing complements Frost's cautious play.

### Ember (Anger)
- **Frost + Ember:** Fire melts ice — Ember can help Frost break out of frozen states, but too much Ember can overwhelm. Tension between the families. Mechanic: Ember's "heat" can remove Frost's "frozen" debuffs, but Frost can reduce Ember's damage output. High-risk/high-reward combos.

### Wren (Grief)
- **Frost + Wren:** Both involve "stuck" states — Frost in fear, Wren in grief. They understand each other. Mechanic: Wren's memory tokens can help Frost recover from "freeze" states. Wren helps Frost carry the weight forward without freezing.

### Tide (Trust)
- **Frost + Tide:** Both involve walls — Frost pushes away from fear, Tide pushes away from hurt. They can teach each other about vulnerability. Mechanic: Tide's "flow" states can convert Frost's defensive stances into more active plays.

### Family Synergies

- **Ice + Shadow:** Both defensive, patient. Wren and Frost together create a slow, grind-it-out playstyle.
- **Ice + Storm:** Ice can contain Storm — Frost can convert Tide's "surge" energy into stored "cold" for later use.
- **Ice + Fire:** Opposites that need balance. The tension is the game.

---

## Mechanical Hooks

- **Fear Tokens:** New resource. Stack up to 5. Each fear token = -1 to all actions. Can be converted to courage 1:1.
- **Courage:** Anti-fear resource. Negates fear tokens. Can be spent for bonus effects.
- **Freeze:** Status that skips your next turn. Different from stun — you can still react to certain things.
- **Frostbite:** DoT (damage over time) that slows healing.

---

## Notes

- This character explores fear — different from Ember's anger, Wren's grief, and Tide's trust issues
- The arc is about *acting despite fear*, not eliminating fear
- "Frost" is the balanced form — not warm, not frozen. Present, aware, capable.
- Defeat as integration: Frost grows by engaging with fear, not by eliminating it
