# Character 06 Concept B — Hearth

**Character Name:** Hearth  
**Emotion Family / Thematic Role:** Warmth — Compassion, care, and boundaries. Hearth is the roster’s dedicated stabilizer: the character who turns kindness from passive healing into active structure.

---

## Concise Background

Hearth is someone who became everyone else’s safe place and quietly disappeared inside that role.

They were the dependable one: the friend who answered late-night calls, the sibling who kept the family running, the partner who smoothed every conflict before it became a fight. For a long time, that looked like love. Eventually it became exhaustion, resentment, and a frightening sense that they no longer knew what they wanted unless someone else needed something first.

Hearth enters the Garden of Echoes not because they failed to care, but because they cared without limits. Their journey is about learning that warmth without boundaries becomes self-erasure — and that real compassion includes protecting the flame as well as sharing it.

---

## 6-Phase Arc Summary

### Phase 1 — Kindling
Hearth begins with reflexive generosity. They help first, ask later, and barely notice the personal cost.

### Phase 2 — Overgrowth
Care spreads everywhere. Hearth is overcommitted, overextended, and keeping too many things alive at once.

### Phase 3 — Smoke
The first signs of burnout appear. Help becomes obligation. Irritation leaks through the kindness.

### Phase 4 — Ash
Hearth hits emotional depletion. They can still function, but the warmth is gone; they are going through motions with nothing left to give.

### Phase 5 — Tend
Hearth starts rebuilding intentionally. Instead of saying yes to everything, they choose where to invest care.

### Phase 6 — Hearth
The final form: steady, protected warmth. Hearth learns that good boundaries do not weaken love — they make it sustainable.

---

## Core Mechanics

### Signature System: **Glow**
A renewable resource representing emotional presence and grounded warmth.

- **Gain Glow** by healing, shielding, or choosing targeted support instead of indiscriminate support.
- **Spend Glow** to amplify cards, prevent ally collapse, or convert defense into momentum.
- Hearth’s late-phase cards reward maintaining a stable Glow band rather than spiking recklessly.

### Secondary System: **Bonds**
Short-term links placed on allies, summons, or even specific cards in hand.

- Bonded targets receive better healing, shielding, or duplicated utility.
- Early phases over-distribute Bonds inefficiently.
- Later phases let Hearth prune Bonds and strengthen a few important connections.

### Tension Mechanic: **Burnout**
Several early and mid-phase cards are intentionally strong but add Burnout.

- Burnout reduces healing output or card efficiency.
- Hearth’s arc is not “heal forever”; it is learning when to stop spending themselves.
- Phase 5-6 cards convert Burnout into insight, efficiency, or protective limits.

---

## Card Direction / Signature Playstyle

Hearth should play like a **support-control character with sustainability decisions**, not a passive healer.

### Early Playstyle
- Wide support, lots of small protection
- Strong emergency saves
- Temptation to overspend resources for everyone
- Feels generous but inefficient

### Mid Playstyle
- Burnout starts punishing panic-healing
- Player has to choose: save everything now, or preserve long-term stability
- Warmth shifts from comfort to discipline

### Late Playstyle
- Precise support, curated Bonds, efficient Glow cycling
- Converts defense into tempo and healing into setup
- Enables allies without disappearing behind them
- Wins by creating a stable board state the enemy cannot crack

### Card Direction Examples
- **Open Door** — Small shield and draw; if target is Bonded, gain Glow
- **Hold Together** — Prevent lethal damage once, gain Burnout
- **Too Much of Me** — Heal all allies, then reduce your next turn’s effectiveness
- **Bank the Fire** — Remove Burnout, gain persistent Glow next turn
- **Say No Kindly** — Cancel an enemy effect; if you declined to heal this turn, gain a bonus
- **Set the Table** — Bond up to two targets, then all Bonded units gain shield
- **Warm Meal** — Heal modestly, remove a debuff, stronger on a small number of targets
- **Hearthlight** — Ultimate state; while active, the first support card each turn is free and excess healing becomes shield

Overall, Hearth’s signature style is **intentional care**: protect what matters, refuse waste, and turn emotional steadiness into board advantage.

## How This Character Complements the Existing 5-Character Roster

### 1. Adds a true Warmth-primary lead
The current lineup covers resentment, anger, grief, trust, and fear. Hearth fills the biggest emotional gap: **compassion and restoration as a primary identity**, not a secondary accent.

### 2. Balances the roster’s emotional weight
Maya, Wren, Frost, and Tide all begin from guarded or painful states. Ember is explosive. Hearth gives the release cast a character whose fantasy is not surviving collapse, but **learning sustainable care**.

### 3. Broadens gameplay beyond aggression or self-management
- **Ember** is pressure and combustion
- **Wren** is memory and burden
- **Tide** is tension and connection
- **Frost** is fear, caution, and courage
- **Maya** is reconciliation through emotional truth
- **Hearth** becomes the roster’s best **stabilizer / protector / anchor**

That creates a cleaner gameplay spread for release.

### 4. Creates strong pairwise contrasts
- **With Ember:** reckless fire vs banked fire
- **With Wren:** carrying grief vs tending what still lives
- **With Tide:** opening up to others vs preserving yourself while caring for others
- **With Frost:** frozen self-protection vs warm, deliberate protection
- **With Maya:** reconciliation after hurt vs care with boundaries before hurt becomes resentment

### 5. Supports the game’s broader thesis
Emotion Cards works best when no single emotional strategy is treated as “correct.” Hearth reinforces that design philosophy by showing that even kindness can become unhealthy when unexamined. Their arc deepens the game’s emotional honesty.

---

## Why Hearth Is a Strong Release Candidate

- Covers the missing Warmth-primary role
- Offers clear support/control gameplay variety
- Keeps the cast emotionally diverse without overlapping Wren or Maya
- Brings a gentler fantasy that still has internal conflict and mechanical bite
- Helps round out the release roster into a more complete emotional spectrum

If Maya is about repairing a fracture, Ember about directing anger, Wren about carrying loss, Tide about risking trust, and Frost about moving despite fear, then **Hearth is about caring without disappearing**.
