## 1. Character Background

### Who is Tide?

Tide is someone who built walls after being betrayed. Not by a stranger — by someone they trusted completely. A friend who revealed a secret. A partner who left without warning. A family member who chose themselves over Tide. The wound isn't fresh anymore, but it never quite healed.

Now Tide exists in a strange middle ground: they're not actively angry, but they've decided it's safer to be alone. They've convinced themselves that needing others is weakness, that vulnerability is just an opening for more pain. Their emotional journey is about rediscovering that opening up isn't the same as being broken — that you can let people in without losing yourself.

### Why This Arc Matters

Trust & Vulnerability is the emotional counterweight to Ember's Anger & Redemption. Where Ember learns that their anger can be a tool for change, Tide learns that their vulnerability can be a strength, not a weakness. Both characters start from pain, but their paths diverge: Ember turns inward (fire burns itself), while Tide pushes outward (storm pushes everything away). Both need to find balance.

### The Storm Family Context

Storms represent Fear, Anxiety, Doubt, and Uncertainty. But storms are also cleansing — they clear the air, they nourish the earth, they create space for new growth. Tide's arc uses Storm mechanics: building charge, releasing tension, unpredictable surges, and eventually — flow.

## 2. Emotional Journey (6 Phases)

### Phase 1: Storm Front

*The walls are up. The sky is dark. No one gets in.*

Tide has withdrawn completely. They're defended, isolated, and proud of it. They tell themselves they don't need anyone. The storm hasn't broken yet — it's building, gathering force, but held in check by sheer will.

**Player Feel:** Defensive, paranoid, strong but lonely. Every card played reinforces isolation.

### Phase 2: Thunder

*The first crack. The anger of "how could they."*

Something triggers Tide's suppressed hurt. The walls are tested. They push back against anyone who gets close, not because they want to fight, but because they're terrified of being hurt again. Thunder is loud — it's the only way to keep people at distance.

**Player Feel:** Aggressive, reactive, lashing out. High tension, high damage, but unsustainable.

### Phase 3: Downpour

*The emotions break through. Overwhelmed, drowning, can't contain anymore.*

The walls crack. Tide can't keep up the pretense anymore. Everything they've been holding back comes pouring out — sadness, anger, fear, longing. It's too much at once. They're soaked in their own feelings, struggling to stay afloat.

**Player Feel:** Chaotic, unpredictable, high variance. Must learn to weather the storm before finding calm.

### Phase 4: Electric

*High tension. Every nerve is exposed. Ready to strike at any sign of threat.*

Tide is raw. Their emotions are close to the surface, ready to react to any perceived danger. They're not sure if they want to fight or flee. This is the most dangerous phase — either they close back up completely, or they take the first tentative step toward trust.

**Player Feel:** High risk/reward, reactive, dependent on reading the opponent's intentions.

### Phase 5: Aftermath

*Exhaustion. The storm has passed. The walls are cracked, maybe crumbling.*

Tide is tired. They can't maintain their defenses forever. In the silence after the storm, they start to wonder: maybe the risk of being hurt is worth the possibility of connection. Small cracks let light in.

**Player Feel:** Transitional, hybrid mechanics emerge, first true ally support cards.

### Phase 6: Tide

*Flowing, adaptable, knowing when to push and when to receive.*

Tide has integrated their vulnerability into strength. They still have walls — but they also have doors. They know when to let people in and when to protect themselves. They're not fixed; they're fluid. Like the tide, they move with the moon, with the pull of others, with the rhythm of connection.

**Player Feel:** Complete, balanced, versatile. Can access all Storm mechanics but with control and intention.

## 3. Card Concepts (20 Cards)

### Design Philosophy

Tide's cards use several unique mechanics:

- **Charge:** Builds up over time, released for powerful effects. Represents emotional tension.
- **Conduct:** Effects that chain between targets. Represents vulnerability spreading.
- **Tidal:** Effects that alternate between powerful and gentle. Represents emotional rhythm.
- **Grounding:** Defensive effects that require connection to others to activate.

## 5. Unique Mechanics

### Charge

A resource that builds up over time, representing emotional tension. Higher Charge = more powerful Storm effects, but also more volatility (risk of self-damage).

- Maximum: 10 Charge
- Some cards build Charge passively
- Some cards spend Charge for power
- Some cards hurt you if you have too much

### Conduct

New keyword: Effects that chain to adjacent targets.

- "Conduct X": Deal X damage/effect to primary target, then half to adjacent targets
- Encourages positioning and ally coordination

### Trust

A buff that enhances an ally's next card.

- "Trust": Next card costs -1 and has +1 effect
- Represents vulnerability: giving someone power over your cards

### Ocean State

The ultimate buff, representing emotional integration:

- Attacks heal allies
- Shields generate Charge
- Support cards are enhanced

## 6. How Tide Complements Maya and Ember

### Tide + Maya

**Theme:** Connection vs. Isolation

Maya's arc is about reconciliation — learning to let someone back in after hurt. Tide's arc is about vulnerability — learning that opening up doesn't mean losing yourself. They're two sides of the same coin:

- **Maya** starts with walls (resentment) and learns to open up
- **Tide** starts with walls (fear) and learns to open up *without losing self*

**Mechanical Synergy:**

- Maya's Warmth cards can apply "Trust" to Tide, enhancing their Storm attacks
- Tide's "Ocean" state makes Maya's healing more powerful
- Both families can trigger Resonance: Warmth + Storm = " Tempest" (chaos but beautiful)

**Narrative Connection:**

- Both characters carry wounds from relationships
- Both learn that vulnerability is strength
- A conversation between them would be: "How did you learn to trust again?" / "How do you keep from losing yourself?"

### Tide + Ember

**Theme:** Vulnerability vs. Anger

Ember's arc is about learning that anger can be a tool, not destruction. Tide's arc is about learning that vulnerability can be a strength, not weakness. They're complementary emotional journeys:

- **Ember** turns pain inward (burns themselves)
- **Tide** pushes pain outward (pushes everyone away)

Both need to find balance: too much Ember = consumed by fire. Too much Tide = frozen in isolation.

**Mechanical Synergy:**

- Fire + Storm = "Storm Fire" (charge up Ember's Heat faster with Tide's Conduct)
- Tide's Charge can fuel Ember's Heat transformation
- Ember's "Wanting to Change" + Tide's "First Drop" = rapid trust building

**Narrative Connection:**

- Both characters use anger/fear to keep people at distance
- Ember learns anger is okay. Tide learns fear is okay.
- A conversation between them: "I burn them." / "I freeze them." / "What if we didn't?"

### All Three Together

**The Complete Emotional Picture:**

- **Maya** = reconciliation (reaching out)
- **Ember** = expression (letting it out)
- **Tide** = reception (letting others in)

Together, they represent the full emotional cycle:

1. **Ember** expresses what's felt (anger, pain)
2. **Tide** processes what's expressed (vulnerability, trust)
3. **Maya** integrates what matters (reconciliation, peace)

A team of all three characters would have:

- Strong offense (Ember)
- Strong defense (Tide)
- Strong support (Maya)
- Access to all four emotion families
- Multiple resonance and transmutation paths

## 7. Emotional Arc Integration

### Phase-by-Phase Design Goals

**Phase 1 (Storm Front):**

- Teach isolation mechanics
- Build Charge = build power
- Show penalties for allies (but make them small enough to tempt)
- Player feels: strong, safe, alone

**Phase 2 (Thunder):**

- Introduce aggressive Storm mechanics
- High damage, high self-risk
- Charge spending = release
- Player feels: powerful but unsustainable

**Phase 3 (Downpour):**

- Introduce chaos mechanics
- AOE and self-damage
- Overwhelm the player with options
- Player feels: need to find calm

**Phase 4 (Electric):**

- Introduce ally support
- First cards where allies are clearly beneficial
- Spark Connection is the key breakthrough
- Player feels: maybe connection isn't bad

**Phase 5 (Aftermath):**

- Hybrid Storm/Warmth cards
- Trust mechanics
- Small, careful vulnerability
- Player feels: growth, hope

**Phase 6 (Tide):**

- Full integration
- Ocean state = ultimate power
- All mechanics available
- Player feels: complete, balanced, ready

### Transition Philosophy

The key transition is from **isolation = power** to **connection = power**:

- Phase 1-2: Isolation is mechanically rewarded
- Phase 3: Isolation starts to hurt
- Phase 4: Allies become beneficial
- Phase 5-6: Connection is strictly better

This mirrors real emotional growth: you have to let go of the safety of isolation to experience the power of connection.

## 8. Balancing Notes

### Early Game (Phase 1-2)

- Charge generation should feel rewarding
- Isolation Pattern is strong but becomes dead later
- Lightning Rod is a trap — self-sacrifice seems noble but limits growth

### Mid Game (Phase 3-4)

- Downpour should feel chaotic — intentional, to push players toward calmer play
- Electric phase is the turning point — new players should feel the shift
- Conductor + Ground Current = strong defensive core

### Late Game (Phase 5-6)

- Dew is the first must-have hybrid card
- Monsoon is a finisher — all-in trust
- Ocean is build-around powerful — if you reach it, you've won

### Charge Balance

- Maximum Charge: 10
- Optimal fighting Charge: 4-6
- Overcharge (7+): Risk of self-damage
- Charge gen cards: Limited to prevent infinite scaling

### Resonance

- Storm resonance (3+ Storm cards): +2 damage, +1 Charge to next card
- Storm + Warmth resonance: "Tempest" — half damage to all enemies, half healing to all allies
