# Wren — Character Specification

## Emotion Cards Game Asset

---

## 1. Character Overview

**Name:** Wren  
**Theme:** Grief & Memory  
**Emotion Family:** Shadow  
**Arc:** Learning to carry loss without being consumed by it

---

## 2. Backstory Summary

Wren lost someone dear — not through death, but through change. The person they loved is still alive, but they're not the same anymore. Maybe they grew apart, maybe they changed, maybe the relationship simply ended. The grief is for someone who's still breathing.

Wren's journey is about understanding that memory isn't a burden — it's a gift. The shadows we carry can either weigh us down or become the wings that lift us. By the end, Wren becomes like the small bird itself — carrying songs from the past, not weighed down but carrying memory forward.

---

## 3. Visual Design Notes

### Appearance

- **Build:** Thin, ethereal — appears slightly translucent at edges
- **Age appearance:** Mid-twenties, but eyes carry centuries of sadness
- **Hair:** Dark, flowing — seems to move even in still air, like shadows
- **Skin tone:** Pale, with a slight blue-gray undertone (shadow family)
- **Eyes:** Large, expressive, with a constant shimmer of unshed tears
- **Notable features:** 
  - Faint, shimmering trails where they walk (like memories lingering)
  - Small shadow-birds that occasionally flutter around them
  - Wears clothing that appears to be from different eras — layered memories
  - A locket they never open — contains a photo they can't bear to look at

### Color Palette

### Personality Indicators

- **Default expression:** Haunted, distant — present but not really there
- **Body language:** Often reaching out to touch something that isn't there
- **Movement:** Slightly floating, not quite grounded in reality
- **Voice:** Soft, dreamy, sometimes echoes slightly

### Design Vibe

- **Art style:** Ethereal, melancholic — like a memory slowly fading
- **Reference:** Spirited Away meets Ghost (the movie) — not scary, just sad-beautiful
- **Mood:** Intangible, bittersweet — grief without horror

## 4. Expression Guide

### Expression: Neutral/Denial
- **Use for:** Character selection, idle state, early journey
- **Eyes:** Slightly unfocused, looking past everything
- **Mouth:** Small, frozen smile — everything's fine
- **Overall mood:** Pretending nothing happened

### Expression: Weight
- **Use for:** Mid-journey, heavy emotional moments
- **Eyes:** Heavier lids, looking down
- **Shoulders:** Slumped, carrying invisible burden
- **Overall mood:** The heaviness of absence

### Expression: Haunting
- **Use for:** Memory-based card moments, haunting visions
- **Eyes:** Wide, seeing things that aren't there
- **Surroundings:** Shadow-birds beginning to appear
- **Overall mood:** Memories that won't let go

### Expression: Wren (Final Form)
- **Use for:** Character culmination, acceptance
- **Eyes:** Clear, peaceful, but still knowing
- **Presence:** Grounded but glowing faintly
- **Shadow-birds:** Small, friendly, perched on shoulders
- **Overall mood:** Carrying memory forward, at peace

## 8. Design Notes

### Why This Look

Wren's design reflects:
1. **Their grief:** Ethereal, slightly intangible — loss that isn't death
2. **Their journey:** From shadow-heavy to light — carrying, not weighed
3. **Their final form:** The wren itself — small bird, big song

### Key Visual Elements

- **Shadow-birds:** Small, dark birds that are friendly not frightening
- **Translucent edges:** Like memories, slightly fading at the borders
- **The locket:** Never opened — the core unprocessed memory
- **Layered clothing:** Different eras, different memories

### Growth Potential

The character design should support visual evolution:
- Early game: Heavier shadows, more ghost-like
- Mid game: Haunted, surrounded by memory-echoes
- Late game: Shadows become wings, bird motifs emerge
- Final form: Peaceful, grounded, carrying (not weighed down)
